Submitted Petition Text:
The problem of substance abuse is so pervasive that only long-term support of locally-based programs can lead to long-term, effective changes. Some positive results have been accomplished in the last decade (see full report elsewhere in ADCA), but much still needs to be done. New and more effective training resources are essential for annual/central conferences. Church leadership at all levels needs to be mobilized to support ministries relating to substance abuse. The spread of HIV/AIDS virus among injected-drug users has reached pandemic proportions. Children and young adults must be reached before they become trapped in cycles of dependency and become victims of AIDS and other infectious diseases. Christians know, and studies show, that the greatest sustained change in people’s lives comes from the kinds of spiritually-based programs that the Church can offer.

Objectives for the Quadrennium

1.  Continue to support drug and alcohol ministries of local congregations through training, networking, and the sharing of resources and skills.
2.  Collaborate with United Methodist initiatives working with defined populations, especially those affected by substance abuse.
3.  Collaborate with United Methodist boards, agencies, and all General Board of Global Ministries' program areas in building expertise in substance abuse-related ministries.
4.  Expand collaboration with central conferences and ecumenical mission partners around the world in ministries related to substance abuse and related violence.
5.  Provide a powerful, effective Christian voice aimed at transforming environments that promote substance abuse and related violence.  

Specific New Program Initiatives for 2005-2008

1.  Hold a workshop for the central conferences of Africa on “Empowerment, Education and Training” to prevent substance abuse among children, youth, and young adults using the peer education model.  
2.  Organize, in collaboration with the General Board of Higher Education and Ministry, a three-day consultation for representatives of the 124 educational institutions related to that agency with the objective of enabling the schools to move from policy to proactive prevention programs.
3.  Develop training modules and a manual to address abuse issues in the congregational health ministries of The United Methodist Church.
4.  Collaborate with General Board of Discipleship to develop age-appropriate Sunday school/ vacation bible school materials promoting prevention and alternative choice options to substance abuse.
5.  Evaluate and expand the peer education program for school children in Ghana, and make it adaptable to other locations. The program should be operative in Liberia and Zimbabwe in 2005 and in four other Africa central conferences by 2008.
  
  
Action Recommendations  
  
1.  That the SPSARV-related  interagency task force  be expanded to include five jurisdictional representatives of Annual Conference Standing Committee on Alcohol and Drugs (SCAD), and that the name of the group be changed to “The Interagency and Standing Committee Task Force.”
2.  That local churches and annual conferences be encouraged to strengthen their role in substance abuse and related violence ministries.  
3.  That the denomination promote the SPSARV Advance Special # 982598-1 as a means through which congregations and individuals can support much-needed tobacco, alcohol, and other drug-related ministries.
