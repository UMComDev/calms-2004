Submitted Petition Text:
WHEREAS, the Council of Bishops of The United Methodist Church organized an Episcopal Initiative on Children and Poverty in 1996, calling on all United Methodist people to the evangelization, resourcing, and reshaping of the denomination in light of the crisis among the world’s children and those oppressed by poverty; and  
WHEREAS, the Council of Bishops in April 2002 recognized that much good work has been accomplished through this Initiative on Children and Poverty but that much remains to be done; and
WHEREAS, the Council of Bishops in April 2002 called United Methodist people and churches to the greater task of establishing relationships and building community with children and the poor; and
WHEREAS, Children and Poverty Area Coordinators at a recent training event in Atlanta, Georgia, have called for the continuation of the Initiative on Children and Poverty, saying, “We are just finding our voice for children and the poor;” and
WHEREAS, the recent downturn in the US economy has generated visible signs of significant pressure on low-income families and communities, i.e. rising unemployment, increased need for food pantry and overnight shelter services, clearly demonstrating that poverty is increasing once again; and
WHEREAS, the people of The United Methodist Church have been energized by and remain committed to mission, ministry, and advocacy with and on behalf of children and those who live in poverty;
Therefore, be it resolved, that the General Conference is called upon to organize the means for the continuation of the Initiative on Children and Poverty so that The United Methodist Church can go forward in word and deed to create community with children and the poor through the redeeming, reconciling, and transforming grace of Jesus Christ.

Submitted ByJ. E. Ryder
IL
Northern Illinois Annual Conference (Annual Conference)
