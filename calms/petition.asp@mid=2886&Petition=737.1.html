Submitted Petition Text:
PROMOTING MINISTRY FOR AND WITH HOMOSEXUALS
WHEREAS, John 3:16-17 tells us that “God so loved the world that He gave His only Son, so that everyone who believes in Him may not perish but have eternal life.  Indeed, God did not send the Son into the world to condemn the world, but in order that the world might be saved through Him.”, and
WHEREAS, The Book of Discipline states:  “Homosexual persons no less than heterosexual persons are individuals of sacred worth.  All persons need the ministry and guidance of the church in their struggles for human fulfillment, as well as the spiritual and emotional care of a fellowship that enables reconciling relationships with God, with others, and with self. . . we affirm that God’s grace is available to all. . . We commit ourselves to be in ministry for and with all persons.” (�161G); and,
WHEREAS, There are persons who are struggling with their feelings and do not want to follow the homosexual lifestyle and there are persons who are presently practicing homosexuals who do not want to stay in this lifestyle, and  
WHEREAS, God has offered a message of love hand hope to anyone wanting to be delivered from bondage of any kind (Luke4:18-19; and Isa. 61:1-2), and

WHEREAS, the General Church agencies have not adequately provided or promoted program resources that facilitate the ministry we have committed ourselves to in accord with the companion statement that “we do not condone the practice of homosexuality and consider this practice incompatible with Christian teaching” (�161G).
Therefore, be it resolved, that the 2004 General Conference assign responsibility to the General Board of Discipleship for providing and/or promoting program resources for United Methodist ministry for and with homosexual persons in keeping with our denominational position on homosexuality (a position now established for 24 years).  These resources should include:
  a.    A clear and grace-filled presentation of United Methodist teaching on human sexuality and homosexuality.
    b.    Understanding of the needs and struggles of persons dealing with homosexual attraction.
c.    Understanding of the needs and struggles of persons who have a family member or friend who is dealing with homosexual attraction.
d.    Support and assistance for those who wish to leave homosexuality.
