Submitted Petition Text:
The Native American Comprehensive Plan of The United Methodist Church shall be developed and directed by the Native American Comprehensive Plan Task Force (NACPTF). The Task Force shall be composed of voting members as follows:
  
� two members from each of the five jurisdictions to ensure Native American input from the local church level. These representatives shall be selected by the College of Bishops, in consultation with Native American United Methodist Churches and Native American United Methodist ministries in the jurisdiction.
    �   two members of the Oklahoma Indian Missionary conference, selected by the conference;
    �   one Native American member of the Alaska Missionary Conference, selected by the conference;
    �   one Native American youth and one Native American young adult, selected by the Native American Comprehensive Plan Task Force;
� one bishop from the Council of Bishops, selected by the Council of Bishops;
    �   two non-staff members of the Native American International Caucus, selected by the caucus; and
� two non-staff members of the National United Methodist Native American Center, selected by the center.
    
In addition, there shall be non-voting members consisting of one representative, as resource persons from each of the following agencies: the General Board of Global Ministries, the General Board of Church and Society, the General Board of Discipleship, the General Board of Higher Education and Ministry, the General Commission on Religion and Race, the General Council on Ministries, the General Commission on the Status and Role of Women, the General Commission on Christian Unity and Interreligious Concerns, United Methodist Communications, and the Commission on United Methodist Men, as well as a representative of Women’s Division of GBGM
  
In the 2005-2008 quadrennium, the Native American Comprehensive Plan (NACP) will intentionally direct its focus around three areas:
   Congregational Development
       Strengthening existing Native congregations, ministries, and fellowships
       Developing new Native congregations, ministries, and fellowships
   Leadership Development
       Strengthening Native leaders for local churches and conferences    
Denominational Presence
          Strengthening contributions of Native leaders, Native congregations, ministries
          and fellowships to The United Methodist Church.
       Continuing to resource the Jurisdictional Native American Committees.
  
A key component in each of the areas is the contributions that Native traditional cultures and Native spiritual expression bring to the mission of the whole church. Native American spirituality is an important component, and one that is woven into each of the three areas.
Congregational Development
  � Rural/reservation/urban planning in cooperation with the General Board of Global Ministries and the annual conferences.
� Renew, Revitalize, Realign (Cooperative Ministries) Grants in cooperation with the General Board of Global Ministries and the conferences.
� Formation Grants working with the conferences and in cooperation with the General Board of Global Ministries.
� Resource consultants to assist in long-range planning with the local church or ministry.
� Small group training in Bible studies, Sunday schools, children’s ministries, youth and elders in cooperation with the General Board of Discipleship.
    
Leadership Development
  � Regional Native Lay Speaking School in cooperation with the General Board of Discipleship.
� Native School of Evangelism in cooperation with the General Board of Discipleship.
� Regional clergy and laity consultations in cooperation with the General Board of Discipleship.
� Training for Native resource consultants.
� College/ University Gathering in cooperation with the General Board of Higher Education and Ministry.
� Native American Campus ministry in cooperation with the General Board of Higher Education and Ministry.
� Culturally-relevant ministries with Native Young adults working in cooperation with the General Board of Global Ministries and the General Board of Discipleship.
� Culturally-relevant material for young adults in cooperation with the General Board of Global Ministries and the General Board of Discipleship.
� Youth leaders/workers training in cooperation with the General Board of Discipleship.
� Culturally-relevant stewardship training in cooperation with the General Board of Discipleship.
� Support for jurisdictional and national EXPLORATION events developed by the General Board of Higher Education and Ministry.
� Seminary grants to Native students between the ages of 18 and 25 years of age.
� Assistance for local churches and conferences in long-range economic development utilizing resource consultants.
� Culturally-relevant youth camps in cooperation with the General Board of Discipleship.
� Lay Ministry training for small membership churches in cooperation with the General Board of Discipleship.
� New ministries and culturally-relevant resources centered on Native American family units (single parents, grandparents raising grandchildren, young parents, etc.) in cooperation with the General Board of Discipleship.
    
  
  
Denominational Presence
  � Support for Jurisdictional Native American ministries working with jurisdictions, annual conferences, and local churches.
� Support for the Conference Committees on Native Ministries.
� National training for the Conference Committees on Native Ministries, along with each of the program agencies.
� A gathering of Native representatives to United Methodist boards and agencies
    �   Addressing issues related to women, youth and elders, through work with the General Board of Church and Society and the Commission on the Status and Role of Women.
      �   A Native American youth and elders consultation.
      
For nearly 500 years, Native Christians have been expressing their love for God in creative and meaningful ways. Our cultures are distinct but equally valid. The Gospel does not lose potency when shared from a Native tradition.
  
It is recommended that the Native American Comprehensive Plan continue into the 2005-2008 quadrennium.
