Submitted Petition Text:
WHEREAS, the movement of the U.S. population out of urban areas and into suburbs and rural areas is increasing; and
WHEREAS, this movement is primarily available only to those people with significant resources; and
WHEREAS, zoning restrictions in suburban and rural areas often prohibit the possibility of low income housing; and
WHEREAS, tax incentives are often used to lure desirable employers to suburban areas where public transportation is not available; and
WHEREAS, this results in urban areas becoming increasingly poor and saddled with aging housing and infrastructure with high levels of unemployment and a reduced tax base; and
WHEREAS, the Social Principles of The United Methodist Church states, “We in the Church have an opportunity and responsibility to help shape the future of urban-suburban life,” and “Christians must judge all programs, including economic and community development, new towns, and urban renewal, by the extent to which they protect and enhance human values, permit personal and political involvement, and make possible neighborhoods open to persons of all races, ages, and income levels” (2000 Book of Discipline ï¿½162 P); and
WHEREAS, Jesus exhibited a preferential option for the poor and the dispossessed;
Therefore be it resolved, that The United Methodist Church supports the increased use of resources to renew and redevelop urban areas, to extend and enhance public transportation, and to mandate affordable housing in all areas of the country;
Be it further resolved, that all local churches are urged to work toward the reduction of urban sprawl as an issue of justice.
