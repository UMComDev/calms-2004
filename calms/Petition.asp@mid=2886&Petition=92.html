Submitted Petition Text:
Add a new resolution to the Book of Resolutions  
  
WHEREAS, Strengthening the Black Church for the 21st  Century (SBC21) Initiative was approved by actions of the 1996 and 2000 General Conferences as a major program initiative; and  
  
WHEREAS, the goal of SBC21 is to offer The United Methodist Church the gift of a transformational learning model that enables vibrant, growing congregations (Congregation Resource Centers) to share their experiences and learnings with other churches (Partner Congregations) seeking growth, vitality and transformation; and  
  
WHEREAS, these church-to-church encounters are very motivational and effective in facilitating new directions and commitments for mission and ministry; and  
  
WHEREAS, the SBC21 model is making significant contributions toward diminishing the differences and building a deeper understanding between large and small, urban and rural congregations; and  
  
WHEREAS, evaluations, written reports, and consultant visits to Partner Congregations reflect an overall assessment of SBC21 as a creative and innovative program; and  
  
WHEREAS, while significant accomplishments have been achieved in the 2001-2004 quadrennium of SBC21, the reality of declining and struggling black congregations still remains.  The task of restoring, reconstructing, redeeming, reconciling, revitalizing and renewing churches for mission and ministry is unfinished; and  
  
WHEREAS, new program developments for 2005-2008 will include development of curriculum for training in Partner Congregations appropriate to the needs and contexts of the communities in which they minister; a study for the solution to the exodus of youth/young adults from African-American churches; the identification and recruitment of African American congregations which are the only, or one of a very few, black churches in an annual conference; the development of program and relationships with congregations of African descent in central conferences and other nations of the global community.
  
Therefore, be it revolved, that the General Council on Ministries endorses, supports, affirms and recommends continuation of the Strengthening the Black Church for the 21st  Century for the 2005-2008 quadrennium and urges that the $2.2 million quadrennial budget request be given funding priority within the 2005-2008 World Service budget.
