Report No. 15
Income from the Board of Trustees


  

The General Council on Finance and Administration, by action of the 1972 General Conference, is the successor to and serves as the Board of Trustees of The United Methodist Church and its predecessor bodies. In this capacity, the General Council on Finance and Administration provides for the management of a portfolio of assets that have been donated to The United Methodist Church for its use, or given to the Board of Trustees to manage for the benefit of a third party. The GCFA maintains records of all distributable income received in the name of the Board of Trustees. Prior General Conferences have directed that the distributable income from undesignated funds be made a part of the World Service receipts for distribution. In the four-year period of 2000 to 2003, a total of $1,620,267 was received and distributed through the World Service Fund.
  
The General Council on Finance and Administration recommends that the Board of Trustees distributable income from undesignated funds be made a part of the World Service receipts for distribution during the 2005-2008 and succeeding quadrennia.

Submitted BySandra Lackore
Evanston IL
