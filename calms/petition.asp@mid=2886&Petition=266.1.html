2005-08 PROPOSALS:  INTO THE FUTURE BY FAITH . . .  
"I planted, Apollos watered, but God gave the growth." 1 Corinthians 3:6 NRSV  
United Methodists have joined hands to collectively plant seeds of ministry through evangelism in the initial phase of Igniting Ministry. With thankfulness to God for the blessings already realized, the General Commission on Communication unanimously proposes the Igniting Ministry media campaign be continued and expanded in the years 2005-08.  
PROPOSAL I:  REACHING MORE OF THE UNCHURCHED  
The effectiveness of spot advertisements increases as viewers experience frequent and repeated exposure to their messages. Continuing our current three-flight effort and augmenting it with a modest increase in carefully targeted new spot placements, can help us invite increasing numbers of people into the community of faith where they can experience the good news of God's love as expressed in Jesus Christ.  
We propose to ...
  Retain three major flights of national television spots at key times (Lent, Back-to-school, Advent) to encourage new visitors;  
    
  • Continue to train and equip conferences, districts and local churches through effective training materials and workshops guided by experienced communicators;  
  • Increase the number of matching grants to conferences, districts and local churches;  
      Continue to lead the United Methodist Open House Month effort each September as a special and visible sign of hospitality in the communities where local churches serve;  
        Expand awareness by purchasing an additional 18 weeks of targeted spot advertising on high-viewership news networks to create a year-round United Methodist presence.  
        Such a sustained effort will ensure that awareness of United Methodism continues and will stimulate interest in visiting a congregation.  
          
        By General Conference 2008, we will ...   
            A.    Reach and maintain 25 percent total awareness among adults ages 25-54  
              B.  Reach and maintain a willingness to attend a United Methodist church at or above 50 percent among non-member adults ages 25-54  
        C.  Exceed and maintain an aggressive 10 percent first-time attendance goal among test churches.  
        D.  Increase total attendance among test churches by 10 percent  (Figures are predicated on substantial increases in first-time attendance and aggressive welcoming efforts by local churches.)  
          PROPOSAL II:  THE YOUTH EXPRESSION  
      
    United Methodists have recognized for many years that relatively lower birth rates among our families and the failure to retain our children’s continuing interest in membership are significant factors of our decades long membership loss. Consequently, today we can identify only approximately 473,253* youth members of United Methodist Youth Fellowship ministries among the more than 8.3 million United Methodists living within jurisdictional conference boundaries.  *Figure from 2001 General Minutes of The United Methodist Church, page 30.  

    The Igniting Ministry Youth Expression will be a grassroots effort to assist young people of United Methodist congregations to actively invite youth to join in their churches’ youth ministries.  It offers coordinated local church resources and advertising materials for local congregations and regional clusters of churches to extend their ministry to greater numbers of youth. It’s an evangelistic sharing of the gospel in a compelling multimedia approach, rather than a television advertising campaign.  
      
    Five Components  
    The Youth Expression’s five coordinated efforts will be designed for use in local churches to reach youth in the name of Jesus Christ:
          1.    Local Church Tools for Youth: Action-based, real, practical and how-to-oriented tools will be developed to help local church youth ministries conduct communitywide efforts to invite young people and welcome them as Christ. In addition, extensive tools will help train youth and youth leaders to implement marketing and advertising strategies in their communities.
          2.    Local Church Advertising Resources:  Music, which is central to youth lives, will be an important foundation in the creative efforts of the Youth Component. Because youth trends change rapidly, all advertising materials will be produced and distributed quickly to local churches.  
          a.             Radio spots
            b.   Print ads
    c.   Posters/environmental graphics  
    d.   A World Wide Web presence for seeker youth  
    e.   Newsletter template for youth ministry programs  
    f.   Graphics, stickers, T-shirts, etc.
              3.    Grants: Matching grants will assist local church youth ministries to launch and sustain strategic marketing/advertising effort in their communities.  
          4.    Web sites: A youth leaders’ site offering resources and an invitational site targeting youth seeking spiritual answers will be developed.  
        5.    Training: Local training will be coordinated and conducted with the General Board of Discipleship.  
    An initiative of the whole church, the Youth Expression is not intended to be a quick fix. It will take time and effort. The goal is to get youth to enter into a United Methodist youth ministry and then to give them a reason to stay.  
      
    WE PROMISE TO MEET THESE GOALS AND OBJECTIVES:
      
    We will invite youth into the community of faith, by:  
          1.    Increasing awareness and recognition of The United Methodist Church’s basic beliefs among youth ages 14-18;  
    2.    Fostering among youth a positive feeling and willingness to visit a local United Methodist church where active youth ministries exist;  
        3.    Renewing a sense of commitment in United Methodist youth.  
        The result will be ...  
      
    Through January 2008, systematic, independent annual tests will analyze the following specific, measurable objectives:  
          1.    Awareness of The United Methodist Church by youth (ages 14-18) will increase by 15 percent.  
        2.    Willingness to attend a United Methodist church will increase by 10 percent.  
        3.    Attendance at United Methodist churches in areas where the campaign is properly implemented will increase by 10 percent.
        4.    Retention among currently attending youth will increase by 10 percent.
