Submitted Petition Text:
Whereas, United Methodist Bishop Joseph Sprague (in his address to students at Ileff School of Theology in Denver, CO, January 2002) made public statements rejecting that Jesus of Nazareth was "born the Christ," rejecting the virgin birth, rejecting the physical resurrection of Jesus Christ from the dead, and rejecting teachings "which hold that Jesus is the only way to God's gift of salvation," and
Whereas, such statements attack the heart of the Christian faith, namely the eternal deity of Christ, the incarnation of God in human flesh, and the resurrection of our Lord, thus placing the teachings of Bishop outside the boundaries of orthodox Christian doctrine, and
Whereas, such statements are in direct conflict with the Scripture, the United Methodist Articles of Religion, all the historic creeds of the Church, and the theological heritage of the Wesleyan tradition, and
Whereas, such statements have done great damage to the United Methodist Church and brought great embarrassment and confusion to our membership, and
Whereas, most of our United Methodist Bishops and other leaders have been silent in their opposition to such teachings, thus adding to the confusion, and
Whereas. charges brought against Bishop Sprague and his teachings were dismissed, falsely implying the acceptance by the United MEthodist Church of such teachings,
Therefore, be it resolved, that the Heber Springs First UMC affirms its commitment to the historic doctrines of the Christian faith as taught in the Scripture and affirmed in the historic creeds of the Church, in the United MEthodist Articles of Religion, and in the srmons of John Wesley.  These basic doctrines include the eternal deity of Jesus Christ, the incarnation of God in human flesh in the person of Jesus.  Jesus' atonement for our sins upon the cross, and His bodily resurrection from the dead.
Furthermore, be it resolved, that any Bishops, pastors, or other leaders in the United Methodist Church who cannot honestly subscribe to these most basic teachings of the Christian faith are unqualified to hold leadership positions in the United Methodist Church.
