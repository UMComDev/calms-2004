Amend ¶255.3 as follows:
  
� 255. Program Ministries-The ministries of the local church ...
3. Civic youth-serving agencies and Scouting ministries offer another setting for ministry to children, youth, their leaders, and their families. These opportunities would include the Boy Scouts of America, Girl Scouts of the USA, Camp Fire Boys and Girls USA, 4-H, or other appropriate national organizations in the central conferences.  The God and Country award program is an essential part of church education and shall be available to all appropriate age-level participants of the local church education program including the church school, youth ministry, and Scouting ministries. The Scouting coordinator shall relate to the church council, the superintendent of the church school, the coordinator of children’s ministries, the coordinator of youth ministries, and the various youth-serving agency structures.
a) Each local church shall have a local church scouting coordinator who shall relate to the church council, the superintendent of the church school, the coordinator of children’s ministries, the coordinator of youth ministries, and all the various youth-serving agency structures. The local church scouting coordinator shall relate to the district scouting coordinator and annual conference scouting coordinator who are resource and training advisors. All churches shall be encouraged to have a signed partnership agreement with all the units of these agencies which are part of their ministry.
b) The local church scouting coordinator has the following responsibilities:
1. To promote scouting ministry as an intentional ministry of the church, providing experiences of nurture, service, mission, and outreach.
2. To coordinate scouting ministries with other programs in the church and to publicize those programs.
3. To encourage dialogue and understanding between pastor and/or church leaders and adult leaders in the scouting ministry.
4. To be a member of the church council and report regularly (� 251.5)
5. To insure that there is a signed partnership agreement with the youth agencies.
6. To promote the God and Country religious education program for all youth in the church. Provide information about the programs available for those of other faiths who are participants in United Methodist Church youth ministries.
7. To work with the pastor in planning and observing Scouting Ministries Sunday or separate special Sundays for each of the youth agencies represented in the churches.
8. To insure that adult leaders become trained for their position, including youth protection, by the youth agencies and the church.
9. To promote recognition awards, including the Bishop’s Award of Excellence for units, the Cross and Flame and God and Service for adult leaders, the Good Samaritan for youth, and other appropriate recognitions.
10. To resource and support the ministries through the youth agencies’ programs, to recruit adult leadership and to recruit children and youth within the community.
11. To annually provide appropriate information to the pastor for the preparation of the local church report to the annual conference.
12. To represent the church on the district scouting committee.
