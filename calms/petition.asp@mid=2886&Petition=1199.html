Submitted Petition Text:
A new, previously nonexistent study committee shall be formed for the purpose of reviewing polity in light of our early tradition employing scripture, reason, experience and tradition as they cast light on our identity.  This committee shall be made of representation from academic disciplines of United Methodist history, and pre-Methodist church history, polity, and doctrine as well as pastors, and United Methodists with proven experience in organizational systems, leadership and management.
    This committee shall work together and each individual will share her or his insights to develop a recommendation for the 2008 General Conference with suggested changes to the Book of Disciple to streamline organization, to clarify roles and responsibilities drawing from our heritage, to make polity familiarity accessible and understandable to all United Methodists, and to strengthen the role of leadership of our denomination that those who are called by God and recognized by the church as such might be freer to exercise leadership and influence and act on guidance of the Holy Spirit.  In addition to suggested changes to the Discipline this group shall also provide a report which compares and contrasts historical United Methodist polity with that of other Christian denominations and with various secular concepts of organization (for instance comparison of franchise verses corporate operated stores in the business realm, forms of national governments, military organization, etc.) in order to help give frames of reference to all United Methodists with which they are already familiar.
Submitted ByShawn Bishop
Mansfield OH
