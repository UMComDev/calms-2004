Submitted Petition Text:
Whereas abortion is the most frequently performed surgery on adults in America (National Center for Health Statistic, Atlanta, GA); and
Whereas women who abort are nearly four times more likely to start abusing drugs or alcohol, when compared to women who have not had abortions("New Study Confirms Link between Abortion and Substance Abuse," The Post-Abortion Review, Fall 1993, p1); and
Whereas 94% of women admit negative psychological effects following abortion, including self-destructive tendencies such as drug or alcohol abuse ("What They Didn't Tell Me About Abortion," Today's Christian Woman, Sept/Oct 1996, p 76); and
Whereas "the average annual suicide rate for women with a history of delivery was only 3.0, while it was 7.8 women with a history of abortion" (Deaths Associated with Pregnancy Outcome:  A Record Linkage of Low Income Women," Southern Medical Journal, August 2002, Vol. 95, No. 8, P. 840); and
Whereas "Thirteen out of 15 American studies [exploring an independent link of abortion with breast cancer] found risk elevations," (The Abortion-Breast Cancer Link:  How Politics Trumped Science and Informed Consent," Journal of American Physicians and Surgeons, Vol. 8, No.2, Summer 2003, p 41 [sources on page 44]);
Therefore, be it resolved, that the 2004 General Conference of the United Methodist Church acknowledge that while women have the legal right to an abortion, some women regret that event later in life; and
Be it further resolved. that the 2004 General Conference of the United Methodist Church urge pastors to become informed about the symptoms and behaviors associated with post-abortion stress; and
Be it further resolved, that the 2004 General Conference of the United Methodist Church encourage local churches to make available contact information for counseling agencies that offer programs to address post-abortion stress for all seeking help.
