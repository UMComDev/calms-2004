Submitted Petition Text:
WHEREAS, since the passage of the Patriot Act, it is now legal for government agents to break into our homes when we are away, conduct a search and keep us from finding out for days, weeks or even months that a warrant was ever issued, and
  
WHEREAS the government can get the courts to rubber stamp their demands for information about the books we read and the materials we borrow from the public library and criminally charge librarians if they tell us, and  
  
WHEREAS the government can obtain our credit reports and other sensitive information with judicial approval and without our consent, and
  
WHEREAS these new powers can be used even in investigations that might have nothing to do with terrorism, because these and other changes in our laws gave government the extraordinary power it sought after September 11, 2001 ( when these changes were rushed through Congress just 45 days after the attacks), without regard for the consequences, and  
  
WHEREAS we know we went far beyond fighting terroism by removing the checks and balances that have helped prevent police and other law-enforcement agents from abusing their power, and
  
WHEREAS we have allowed government agents to violate our civil liberties, tapping deep into the private lives of innocent Americans, and
  
WHEREAS just when we thought it couldn't get worse, the government planned a second expansion-of-powers bill (the Domestic Security Enhancement Act of 2003, or "Patriot Act II") that would go even further, incredibly granting government agents even broader authority to search our homes, learn what we read, find out where we vacation, and monitor what drugs our doctors prescribe, and
  
WHEREAS this would allow:  the government to strip Americans of their citizenship if they have provided support to organizations labeled as "terrorist" by the government, even if they know nothing of the organizations' alleged links to terrorism; the expansion of home searches and wiretaps without a warrant; secret arrests; new immunity for federal agents who conduct illegal surveillance with the approval of high-ranking executive branch officials (this provision would have protected the Nixon-era wiretappers), therefore,
  
BE IT RESOLVED that the General Conference of The United Methodist Church declares that when our government attacks core liberties, even in the name of antiterrorist national security, it goes too far, and  
  
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that, when our government removes vital checks and balances that have kept us safe for more than 220 years, it causes permanent damage to civil liberties and civil rights, and
  
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that this General Conference calls upon the Congress, before it goes any further, to investigate how the powers it has already given the government are being used, or abused, and  
  
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that this General Conference calls upon the Congress to protect core American values by taking action now to prevent the further expansion of the government's spy powers.
