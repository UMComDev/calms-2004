Submitted Petition Text:
Whereas, at present time The United Methodist Church, hereinafter referred to as the "church", is an unincorporated association of persons of like minds, and is therefore a non-entity unable to hold title to property real, personal, or mixed in its own name.
It is essential that the church itself have the ability to hold, control, and use donations and gifts of money, securities, and other assets tangible and intangible for the benefit of the whole of the church and those persons who align, associate, and call or regard themselves as members of The United Methodist Church.
Be it therefore resolved by the General Conference that:  
In order to assure that The United Methodist Church becomes a legal entity so that the church has control over all of its assets that have been donated, given, or otherwise acquired by it in any manner and to follow the intention of the donors of said property to donate directly to the church so that any such property goes to the church itself and not to another corporation or other body.  The name of the incorporated church shall be:  The United Methodist Church, Incorporated, USA.
PURPOSE
The object and purpose of taking this action is to protect those who claim authority in The United Methodist Church including but not limited to all the active members of the Council of Bishops from individual and collective personal responsibility for the liabilities of the church, and to assure donors that their donations go directly to the church as is or was the intent of the donors.
INCORPORATION
The United Methodist Church shall be incorporated under the laws of the District of Columbia, or in any other state that the church shall deem through legal counsel to be the most appropriate or the most advantageous to the church, as determined by the Board of Directors of the General Council on Finance and Administration, hereinafter referred to as the Council.
FUNDING
The church shall receive all gifts, bequests, donations, assets and property of all kinds in the name of The United Methodist Church, Inc., USA.  Any and all assets donated, given or acquired in the name of The United Methodist Church, UMC, and held at the present time for the benefit of the church by any agency, board, general board, commission, or any other entity or person including the title to the "Methodist Buildings" at 100 Maryland Avenue, and 115 Maryland Avenue, Washington, DC and all appurtenances thereto appertaining shall be transferred to the church, and title to any real property so transferred shall be recorded in the District of Columbia with the payment to each present title holders the sum of $1.00 and other good and valuable consideration.  Transferees of title shall include, but not limited to, the General Board of Church and Society (GBCS), and the General Council on Finance and Administration (GCFA).  The church shall be authorized to solicit and create special funds; to receive gifts and bequests; to hold properties and securities in trust; and to administer all those financial affairs in accordance with its own rules and the provisions of the Discipline, Social Principles and Resolutions of The United Methodist Church.
Board of Directors
The Board of Directors shall consist of thirty-six (36 persons) and the officers shall be a President, Vice President, Secretary, and Treasurer.  The President shall be a member of the laity, and no Bishop of The United Methodist Church shall serve as President at any time.  The total number of Bishops serving on said Board of Directors or as officers of the corporation, other than as President shall be limited to seven (7) Bishops, or one for each District whichever is greater.  A temporary Board of Directors shall be appointed by the 2004 General Conference and shall serve only for organizational purposes, for a period not to exceed 180 days.  After the expiration of that time, the Board of Directors shall be elected by a majority of the pastors of each local UMC church in each District present and voting by proxy or in person with each having one vote.  The term in office shall be four (4) years, and no member of the Board shall serve for more than two terms.  In the event of a vacancy in Board membership, it shall be filled in accordance with the principles outlined in Paragraph 712 of the General Provisions of the Discipline (1996).
MEETINGS AND VOTING
The Board shall meet at least six (6) times per annum, or more often at reasonable times selected at the pleasure of the President, or at a call by a majority of the Board of Directors.  A majority shall be at least nineteen (19) votes.  Meetings may be held by a conference telephone call, or at a place and time mutually agreeable to at least 15 members of the Board of Directors or in both manner.  Voting may be done by electronic device, i.e. a computer program set up to assure that the person voting is the individual qualified to vote.
STAFF
The United Methodist Church, Inc. USA, shall be authorized to hire and fire a General Secretary or Associate Secretaries, as well as other personnel needed and commensurate with its budget and its needs to carry out its purpose.  It shall be otherwise organized and operate according to the general provisions of the Discipline, except where they are in conflict with the provisions herein adopted.
