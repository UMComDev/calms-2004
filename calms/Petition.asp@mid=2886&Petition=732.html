Submitted Petition Text:
As we enter into a new centennial of Korean immigration and United Methodist mission in our land, we heed the call of God to create, nurture and mobilize communities of faith for evangelism, ministries of healing and wholeness, bridge-building, social justice, community outreach, and connectional ministry. In response to that call of God, the Korean American National Plan presents the following mission strategy for the 2005-2008 quadrennium with new visions and renewed faith in God for the future. It is our hope that this strategy will upgrade and expand the previous quadrennium’s National Plan.

We recommend the following three vision areas for our collective effort:
  •   Servant-Leadership Formation
  •   Next Generation Ministries
  •     Congregational Development, Nurture, and Redevelopment
          
        Based on these vision areas, we highlight the following specific strategies for implementation:

    Servant-Leadership Formation
      
    Forming spiritual leaders for ministry with a servant’s heart is an important focus of the overall vision of the National Plan. It was discovered early on in the implementation of the National Plan, that servant-leadership formation needs to have a broad scope in its approach since the leadership of Korean ministry has become increasingly diverse in terms of gender, generation, age, ministry setting, leadership style and cultural orientation. Therefore, it is recommended that a multi-faceted approach for servant-leadership formation be undertaken in the following ways:
    1. A comprehensive and focused strategy for servant-leadership formation for the pastors who are directly involved with the Korean language ministry.
    2. Servant-leadership training for the laity of all ages in order to vitalize the ministries of the KUMC and UMC in local and broader settings.
    3. An effective mentoring program for young Korean pastors and new mission congregations.
    4. An effective and active strategy to develop servant-leadership among the Korean American clergywomen for the Korean ministry and cross-cultural ministries in The United Methodist Church.
    5. A specific servant-leadership formation program to support and empower a growing number of Korean American ministers serving in cross-cultural appointments and bi-cultural ministries in The UMC.
    6. A servant-leadership formation training for both clergy and laity to build bridges among those who come from different age, cultural, theological, and ministerial backgrounds.
        7. Cultural orientation and sensitivity training for district superintendents and conference ministerial staff, who are not familiar with the Korean culture and customs.
        
    It must be emphasized that the Korean American leadership will benefit from the training on new leadership style, cross-cultural communication, intergenerational studies, the United Methodist polity and connectional system. Such training will be undergirded by the spiritual vitality of the Korean American United Methodists. At the same time, it is equally important that the leadership of annual conferences and other extended United Methodist connections continue to deepen their appreciation and understanding of their Korean American brothers and sisters for more holistic partnership in ministry.
      
    Next Generation Ministries

    The Korean American United Methodist community is in a time of critical transition.  It seeks to find the most effective ways to foster and develop Next Generation ministries, as well as servant-leadership. Investing time, energy and resources for these innovative ministries is a top priority of the National Plan for the sake of the present and future of the church.

    “The silent exodus syndrome,” which was mentioned in the report of the General Conference Task Force on Korean American Ministries to the 2000 General Conference, seems to be taking place, not only among many Korean American youth and young adults, but also among the English Ministry pastors. Several of them are leaving their Next Generation ministries due to burn-out and disappointments. It has also been realized that most of the current Next Generation ministries are predominantly small in membership and need long-term strategies for vitalization and revitalization.
      
    An important trend is that a large number of Next Generation churches are also interested in developing Campus Ministries in their respective areas. It is quite conceivable, since most of the Next Generation Ministries are comprised of college students and young adults, they are gradually maturing in terms of age and are becoming intergenerational themselves.  
      
    Another important trend is that most of the current Next Generation churches are fast becoming multi-racial and multi-cultural as non-Korean laity are joining their congregations. Also, a growing number of the 2nd  and 3rd  generation Korean American young people are marrying non-Koreans.
      
    These important trends of the present generation inspire the Korean American National Plan to draw a different picture of strategic focus for the 2005-2008 quadrennium. At the same time, it calls for vigorous recruitment plans to recruit and develop a new generation of spiritual servant-leaders for Korean American ministries in The UMC. In this regard, the National Plan recommends that the following strategies be developed and implemented:
      
    1.    Continue to vitalize the Transgeneration Ministry of The UMC, the national coordinating body for Next Generation clergy and laity, which has been in existence since 1982.
    2.    Strengthen the summer internship program for college students.
          3.   Call upon UM-related seminaries to develop recruitment programs, provide scholarships, and design exciting education curriculum to attract Next Generation Korean American persons for ministry.
    4.   Continue to nurture the national and regional network groups for the Next Generation pastors and seminarians, and campus ministries.
    5.   Vitalize the “Flagship Next Generation English Ministry Churches Project,” a congregational development training program for selected Next Generation churches which, in turn, can help strengthen other Next Generation ministries.
    6.   Develop a workable mentoring program for young Next Generation pastors with a particular emphasis on self-care, practical ministry, and servant-leadership formation.
    7.   Develop an effective and innovative lay leadership program for Next Generation Ministries.
          
    Congregational Development, Nurture and Redevelopment

    During the first quadrennium of existence of the United Methodist Council of Korean American Ministries, much of the energy and resources have been focused on developing new congregations. For the next quadrennium, it is recommended that more emphasis be placed on nurturing the existing mission congregations toward becoming self-supporting and actively participating in and supporting the overall mission and ministry of The UMC.  It is further recommended that new ministries be continually developed in a more efficient and effective manner. In response to the needs of many chartered KUMCs who are seeking to revitalize their ministries, an additional new focus under the heading “Redevelopment” is proposed.

    The following specific steps are recommended:

    1.    Develop 15 new Korean language ministries in full partnership with annual conferences, Korean Missions and Korean UMCs.
    2.    Nurture these new mission congregations and existing mission congregations to become self-sustaining within a short period of time.
          3.   Provide direct support to local KUMCs for their innovative redevelopment ministries to grow both in their congregational life and mission.
    4.   Provide resources to mobilize Korean American churches to develop community outreach and justice ministry opportunities.  
          
    ACTION PLAN FOR 2005-2008 QUADRENNIUM

    The Action Plan for the Korean American National Plan seeks to fully utilize the many spiritual gifts and resources that the Korean American United Methodist community brings to the common table of The UMC:  stewardship and tithing, fervent prayer, vital spirituality, class meetings, and mission. It also seeks to strengthen ministries in the areas of outreach, social witness, justice ministry, and participation in the UMC connectional system. With these objectives in mind, and understanding that it takes the whole UMC to make the Korean American National Plan work, the following specific goals for the Action Plan and recommended collaborative work with annual conferences and general program agencies are outlined below:

       Goal 1: Implement an effective servant-leadership formation strategy and program for clergy, professional staff and lay leaders of first and next generations with emphasis on spiritual servant-leadership formation, the United Methodist connection, community outreach and justice ministry, mission interpretation, intergenerational partnership, and cross-cultural communication.
      
      
  •   COUNCIL OF BISHOPS:  Develop an effective process of cross-conference and cross-jurisdictional appointments of clergymen, clergywomen and next generation ministry pastors.
  •   GCOM and COUNCIL OF BISHOPS:  New District Superintendents and Connectional Ministries Directors Training for cultural sensitivity regarding the Korean American United Methodist community.
  •    GBHEM:  Design and facilitate culturally relevant comprehensive continuing education opportunities for Korean American United Methodist clergy.
  •    GBOD:  Design and facilitate dynamic comprehensive lay leadership training for strengthening Korean American United Methodist congregations’ mission and ministry.
  •    GBCS:  Resource Korean American United Methodist churches for community outreach and justice ministry.
  •    GCORR:  Work with the Korean American UM community to bring about “bridge-building ministry” opportunities with other races and ethnic groups in the United States.
          
       Goal 2: Vitalize a leadership formation program and systematic integration strategy of Korean American clergywomen for ministry in KUMCs, as well as the entire UMC denomination.
      
      
  •   COUNCIL OF BISHOPS:  Provide leadership in appointing clergywomen to vital Korean American United Methodist churches and nurturing that process for both clergy and congregations.
  •   GCOSRW:  Provide direct support in the UM Council on Korean American Ministries’ effort to advocate Korean American clergywomen’s ministry in Korean American UMCs and develop leadership skills among clergywomen.
  •   GBHEM:  Provide staff support for developing models of clergywomen’s ministry and offering training for clergywomen.
          
       Goal 3: Strengthen existing next generation congregations and establish 15 new next generation congregations and campus ministries.
      
      
  •   ANNUAL CONFERENCES:  Develop strategy and mobilize support to start new English ministries, including campus ministries.
  •   NEXT GENERATION ENGLISH MINISTRY RESOURCE CENTER AT WESLEY THEOLOGICAL SEMINARY, D.C.:  Coordinate with the UM Council on Korean American Ministries in data gathering, research, analyzing and creating new models/paradigms for developing and nurturing English Ministries for growth.  
  •    GBOD: Implement the “Flagship Church Project” which aims to develop vital pastoral leadership and congregations to support other English ministries.
          
       Goal 4: Implement a productive strategy to recruit next generation young men and women to be trained in UM-related seminaries for ordination in The UMC.
      
      
  •   GBHEM:  Work with the UM Council on Korean American Ministries in developing a viable recruitment strategy for next generation young men and women for UM ministry.
  •   THIRTEEN UM-RELATED SEMINARIES:  Provide scholarships and exciting education curriculum to attract next generation young men and women for ministry.
          
       Goal 5: Nurture existing Korean American mission congregations to become self-sufficient, strengthen chartered KUMCs, and establish 15 new Korean congregations.
      
      
  •   ANNUAL CONFERENCES:  Organize Conference Committees on Strengthening Korean Ministries and develop strategies toward the goal; the UM Council on Korean American Ministries will grant seed money for up to 20 annual conferences during the 2005-2008 quadrennium.
  •   GBGM:  Work with the Council to develop effective ways to nurture and support the existing Korean American mission congregations and mission superintendents/directors.
  •   COUNCIL OF BISHOPS, GBGM, GBHEM, and GCORR: Work with the Council to interpret and promote the ministries of Korean mission pastors, develop a process for them to enter into full ministerial membership within The UMC, and provide coordinated leadership to enhance cross jurisdictional itineration of these mission pastors.
  •    COUNCIL OF BISHOPS, GBGM and KMC:  Develop an effective partnership for shared mission with our KMC brothers and sisters in Korea, as well as in the United States.
  •    JURISDICTIONAL CONFERENCES:  Promote and support the work of the Jurisdictional Korean Missions.
          
       Goal 6: Expand culturally relevant resource materials and national data on Korean American UM churches and pastors.

      
  •   UM PUBLISHING HOUSE, GBOD, and UPPER ROOM: Develop ministry related materials for both Korean-speaking and English-speaking congregations of the Korean UM community.
  •   UMCOM:  Develop an English version of TV spots and provide training for the Next Generation churches.
  •    UMCOM:  Expand national data and website on KUMCs.
          
    ORGANIZATION

    In order to effectively carry out the work of the Action Plan during the 2005-2008 quadrennium, the continuation of the United Methodist Council on Korean American Ministries is recommended. For the optimum outcome of the National Plan’s work, it is further recommended that the Council be reduced in size in order to organize as efficiently and effectively as possible while maintaining good representation and balance.  The Council will continue to strengthen its close partnerships with annual conferences and general program agencies through mutual sharing of leadership and resources.
