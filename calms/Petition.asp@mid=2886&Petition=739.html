Submitted Petition Text:
WHEREAS, “the covenant of marriage was established by God, who created us male and female for each other” (from our United Methodist Service of Christian Marriage); and,  
WHEREAS, our church states, “We affirm the sanctity of the marriage covenant that is expressed in love, mutual support, personal commitment, and shared fidelity between a man and a woman. We believe that God’s blessing rests upon such marriage” (�161C); and,  
WHEREAS, our church is opposed to homosexual unions (�332.6); and,  
WHEREAS, studies have consistently shown that marriage is a key to reducing high levels of youth crime and child poverty, caused by the epidemic of fatherless families in America; and,  
WHEREAS, a very broad interfaith coalition of support, including bi-partisan support in Congress, is taking shape in this country to support an amendment to the United States Constitution regarding the nature of marriage; and,  
Whereas, the proposed Federal Marriage Amendment simply states (in its entirety):  
    “Marriage in the United States shall consist only of the union of a man and a woman.  Neither this constitution or the constitution of any state, nor state or federal law, shall be construed to require that marital status or the legal incidents thereof be conferred upon unmarried couples or groups.”
Therefore be it resolved, that the 2004 General Conference go on record as supporting this Federal Marriage Amendment.
