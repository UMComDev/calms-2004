Submitted Petition Text:
With more than ten years of ministry (1992 to present)--filled with new learnings and challenges, local churches discovering capacities heretofore unknown, individuals finding self-worth within themselves catapulting them to new levels of spiritual awareness and involvement, and communities finding the strength and resolve from within to become what they are capable of becoming--Communities of Shalom has met and exceeded the goals of its mandate. (See full report elsewhere in ADCA).
  
  A “Ten Year Evaluation” has been completed that chronicles the initiative’s steps from its inception to its present status. The evaluation has affirmed the core structure of Communities of Shalom and the success of this structure in preparing church and community leadership to effect systemic change towards eradicating societal and spiritual inequities responsible for so many maladies. The evaluation has further identified places where the sustenance of Shalom ministries must be addressed to stabilize and advance its presence in communities and local churches.
  
This recommendation to the 2004 General Conference proposes that primary emphasis in the next quadrennium be given to the transition of Communities of Shalom from a church-wide initiative to an annual conference-driven leadership and structural design such that the Shalom ministry will assume a life of its own. To continue the work of Communities of Shalom through this critical period of transition, the following support is requested:
