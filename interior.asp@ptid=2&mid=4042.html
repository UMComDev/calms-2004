Annual conferences say goodbye to bishops, approve budgets

April 7, 2004

By United Methodist News Service

Some United Methodist annual (regional) conferences meeting this spring and summer will say goodbye to retiring bishops and become better acquainted with the candidates they are endorsing for the episcopacy.

The 2004 annual conference sessions will be the last for 20 bishops in the United States. They will retire from active service after jurisdictional conferences in July elect their successors. Retiring are 12 white men, two white women, four African-American men, one Asian-American man and one Hispanic man. Four bishops in the central conferences - regional units outside the United States - are also retiring this year.

The Red Bird Missionary Conference, meeting May 14-15 in Frakes, Ky., will be the first of 63 U.S. conferences to conduct sessions this spring. The final U.S. conference gathering will be East Ohio, meeting June 21-25 in Lakeside.

Some annual conference sessions outside the United States were held in March, and other meetings will occur through December. Meeting dates for some conferences in Africa and Europe are incomplete.

Annual conference members, including lay and clergy representatives from every local church, will also approve budgets, receive reports from conference boards and agencies, adopt programs of mission and ministry, and pass resolutions.

A bishop will preside over each conference session. One-year appointments of all clergy members of the conference will be announced, new deacons and elders will be ordained, and candidates for ordination will be approved.
