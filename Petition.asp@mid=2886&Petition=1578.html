    The General Council on Finance and Administration fulfills a wide variety of oversight responsibilities within The United Methodist Church as directed by the General Conference and listed in The Book of Discipline 2000. These ongoing responsibilities address issues in the financial, legal, risk management, and administrative areas. The council performs the custodial trustee function of the denomination. In conjunction and cooperation with the other councils, the GCFA provides for the continued direction of the Church between the quadrennial sessions of the General Conference.
  
In living out its ministry of administration the council includes in its ongoing responsibilities support of several United Methodist associations. These include the United Methodist Association of Church Business Administrators, the Professional Association of United Methodist Church Secretaries, the United Methodist Association of Annual Conference Computer Administrators, and the National Association of Commissions on Equitable Compensation of The United Methodist Church. The GCFA provides guidance, consultation and assistance to these associations in a variety of ways including training programs and workshops, establishment of professional standards, certification, informational resources and staff support.  
  
Through its Risk Management Department, the council institutes, maintains, and manages an insurance program that is available to local churches and annual conferences. The Legal Department conducts legal forums for annual conference chancellors, develops resources to assist them with their work, and helps establish linkages for the chancellors to resource each other on behalf of the Church. Along with the General Board of Discipleship the council collaborates to resource United Methodist foundations in the various annual conferences.
  
GCFA is currently located in Evanston, Illinois and Nashville, Tennessee. The Nashville office has assumed additional staffing responsibilities in recent years due to the proximity to four of our agencies. GCFA is currently evaluating the advisability of consolidating its offices into the Nashville location.
  
The Benefit Trust is a significant income stream for GCFA, as it is for all of the agencies receiving general Church funds. The distributions from the Benefit Trust will be reduced from 8% (2001-2004) to 4% (2005-2008) per action of the 2000 General Conference. As with the other agencies, the GCFA budget recommendation reflects the need for increased apportionments to cover the reduced distributions.
