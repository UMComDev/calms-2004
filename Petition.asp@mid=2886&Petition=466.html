Submitted Petition Text:
WHEREAS The Book of Discipline (�1004) mandates the General Board of Church and Society to “seek the implementation of the Social Principles and other policy statements of the General Conference on Christian Social Concern” and  
WHEREAS, the Social Principles (�162J) explicitly reaffirms “our long standing support of abstinence from alcohol as a faithful witness to God’s liberating and redeeming love for persons...  W e support educational programs encouraging abstinence from such use,” and  
WHEREAS, in fulfilling its disciplinary charge the General Board of Church and Society has created and empowered a Work Area on Alcohol and Other Addictions, with full-time staff, and
WHEREAS, in 1989 the Bishops' Initiative on Substance Abuse and Related Violence led by Bishop Felton May was established, staffed, and managed by the General Board of Church and Society, and
WHEREAS, in 1992 the General Conference established the Special Program for Substance Abuse and Related Violence (“SPSARV”) and directed that it be an interagency program whose staff reported to and was managed cooperatively by the General Board of Church and Society and the General Board of Global Ministries, and  
WHEREAS, The Book of Discipline defines a program as “an ongoing or special activity designed and implemented to fulfill a basic disciplinary responsibility of a general agency”. (�703.11), and
WHEREAS, The United Methodist Church has historically entrusted the General Board of Church and Society and its predecessor with its ministry to the world about alcohol, tobacco, and other addictive substances, and  
WHEREAS, the General Board of Church and Society continues its comprehensive ministry of education about the dangers of and treatment for alcohol and other drug abuse and its advocacy for the regulation and taxation of alcohol, legal and illegal drugs, and tobacco, and  
WHEREAS, at present SPSARV is hindered by a lack of a unified approach to the issues it is charged to implement.
WHEREAS, the General Board of Church and Society is charged with conducting “a program of research, education, and action on the wide range of [social justice] issues that confront the Church.” (�1004)  
Therefore, be it resolved, that the 2004 General Conference of The United Methodist Church create a unified approach to the social justice issues of alcohol and other drugs by transferring the responsibility for the interagency SPSARV program and its funding to the General Board of Church and Society commencing immediately following General Conference 2004, or on or before July 31, 2004.
