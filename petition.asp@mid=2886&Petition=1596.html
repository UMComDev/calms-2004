Report No. 16

REPORT OF THE ADVANCE FOR CHRIST AND HIS CHURCH TO THE 2004
GENERAL CONFERENCE

Hebrews 11 speaks to the “stick-to-it-ness” of the faithful who preceded us centuries ago in
difficult times.
  
The Advance video premiered at the 2000 General Conference was entitled “So Great a Cloud of
Witnesses.” This title is from the larger passage of Hebrews 12:1-2: “Therefore, since we are
surrounded by so great a cloud of witnesses, let us also lay aside every weight and sin which
clings so closely, and let us run with perseverance the race that is set before us, looking to Jesus
the pioneer and perfecter of our faith.”
  
This millennium likewise challenges the perseverance of the faithful. We experience economic
“dis-ease.” We experience the destruction of the World Trade Center, Al Qaeda, the Taliban,
Afghanistan, Iraq, homeland security and other issues and events affecting people who are urban
and rural, rich and poor, male and female, young and old.
  
Yet, warmhearted United Methodists continue to go “the second mile” in mission and ministry
through The Advance for Christ and his Church. Mission Personnel, an Advance priority,
includes many creative ways people answer God’s call to “go...and make disciples of all
nations...”(Matthew 27:19). Young Adult Missioners, US-2’s (recent college graduates),
Missioners of Hope, Mission Interns, Global Mission Partners, Hispanic Plan Missionaries, 10-
10-10 Missionaries, Korean-American Mission Pastors, Deaconesses, International Persons in
Mission, Church and Community Workers, and missionaries serving inside and outside the
United States share the word of God, in many languages, in more than 60 counties.

Through the Advance, love and care is provided to our children and youth in more than 120
community centers and residences. Sanctuaries and church buildings are constructed to
appropriately reflect people’s unique geographical environments and cultures. On all continents,
through face-to-face evangelism, people who have never heard the gospel are educated and
nurtured into United Methodism.
  
In an age when rapid social and economic change destabilizes communities, towns and cities, our
local congregations and annual conferences have united their gifts and their presence to provide
ministries of health, wholeness, leadership and economic development, justice, and literacy.
With the cooperation of the General Board of Global Ministries and United Methodist
Communications, The Advance for Christ and His Church produces print, audio, and web
resources in English, Spanish and Korean languages.

Even as economic uncertainty and catastrophic events define the mass media’s daily message,
United Methodists are undaunted in their ministries of compassion. We assist people rebuilding
their lives in the countries that were once the Soviet Union. We provide shelter to people made
homeless by floods, earthquakes, drought, and war. We comfort and heal people victimized by
land mines or ravaged by disease. We feed the hungry. We clothe the naked. We live the gospel.
Whether terror strikes within our own borders or on any other continent, United Methodists join
with other peace seekers in offering healing and reconciliation to persons hurting emotionally and
physically, regardless of their religion or country of origin.
