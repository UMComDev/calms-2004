Submitted Petition Text:
WHEREAS,  Jesus healed the sick and the stigmatized; and  
WHEREAS, His healing was generally gently given and voluntarily received; and  
WHEREAS, The Church seeks to follow His example in its worldwide healing ministry; and  
WHEREAS, Tens of millions are said to bear the stigma of HIV/AIDS,
particularly in Africa; and  
WHEREAS, The consequences can include childlessness and lifelong drug
treatment, which treatments can have severe side effects; and  
WHEREAS, Some, including pregnant women and new mothers, have been
forced to take or give to their children such drug
Treatments against their will, with some losing custody of their children;
and  
WHEREAS, Some scientists and officials have raised concerns, some of
which can be found through web site
http://www.aidspetition.org, regarding HIV/AIDS assumptions, causes,
tests and treatments; and  
WHEREAS, Some in Africa have suggested that hunger and poverty may be
the likely causes of illness there; and  
        WHEREAS, None of those said to have HIV or AIDS have been cured by drug treatments.  
        Therefore be it resolved, that the General Conference of The United Methodist Church [hereinafter "the Church"] urge the United States government and the United Nations to convene promptly an open forum at which these concerns and other concerns with current HIV/AIDS policy can be debated publicly, open to all views and willing to consider alternate approaches and causes, with the objective of finding the best possible outcome for those said to have HIV or AIDS, and for the broader society as well.
Therefore be it further resolved, that the Church itself deliberate on these questions, so that the Church may decide how best, in Christ’s spirit, to help those bearing the stigma of HIV, and those who are sick.
                
Submitted ByFrank Stoppenbach
Red Hook NY
