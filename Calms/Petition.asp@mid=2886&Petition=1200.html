Submitted Petition Text:
Because of social and cultural changes and in response to Section VIII, paragraph 434 (Appointment Making: Frequency) which states, While the bishop shall report all pastoral appointments to each regular session of an annual conference, appointments to charges may be made at any time deemed advisable by the bishop and cabinet. Appointments are made with the expectation that the length of pastorates shall respond to the long-term pastoral needs of charges, communities, and pastors. The bishop and cabinet should work toward longer tenure in local church appointments to facilitate a more effective ministry, a new, previously non-existent study committee shall be formed to report back with recommended changes to the Book of Discipline at the 2008 General Conference.  This group shall elaborate on the nature of both “appointment” and “itineracy.”  The implication of “appointment” shall be considered and drawn out for general education of United Methodists and a discussion of an emphasis of “appointment” over the concept of “itineracy.”  A historical study of the nature of “appointment” and its emphasis and implications in the historical church from the beginning with biblical support shall be included.  Furthermore, a consideration of “itineracy” shall be considered throughout Christian history, in the early Methodist movement in England, and in early American Methodism (not to exclude its spread around the world).  The principles for the various expressions shall be drawn out as they shed light on the goal of “itineracy” on each given cultural situation.  Discussion of itineracy in a para-church movement shall be contrasted with concepts in a formal ecclesial body.  Several models of “new-itineracy” may be shared which seek to be faithful to principles of our past more than forms (e.g. pulpit itineracy by a pastoral team, pastoral teams assigned to a circuit with frequent rotation but long term appointment to the circuit [when practical and fruitful], regional churches, itineracy of roles and responsibilities of long-term pastor teams, etc.).

Mansfield OH
