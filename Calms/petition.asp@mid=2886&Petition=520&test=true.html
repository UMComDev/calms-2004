Submitted Petition Text:
WHEREAS, it appears that the United Methodist Church has abandoned the city, and its servants and those being served, the  men, women, and children that dwell in the city; and  
Whereas, our children are dying without knowing the love of Jesus Christ; and
WHEREAS, the situation in our urban areas is of crisis proportions, with our church losing not only individual members but congregations as well; and  
WHEREAS, the continuation could soon leave little or no effective United Methodist presence in many of our Urban Areas; and
WHEREAS, if one of our Urban churches suffers in membership decline and struggles to minister to a challenging community, then the whole United Methodist church suffers; and
WHEREAS, almost all of us seek and depend on the many resources of the city; and
WHEREAS, the city is the site of tremendous poverty, powerlessness, and pain; and
WHEREAS, we are instructed by the ‘Word of GOD” to provided a ministry of presence and reconciliation to men, women, and children, and to peoples of all economic, educational, cultural and social stations, and to people of diverse and alternative lifestyles; and
WHEREAS, we must come to see ourselves in partnership with “Our Lord and Savior Jesus the Christ, who walked the streets of Jerusalem, in ministry and mission in the city in the world; and to continue to support ministries of Urban churches and agencies as a body, as a part of our ministry to the whole world; and
WHEREAS, we are a people with a vision for the future and an awareness of the past, compassion for today, who choose to remain in our cities regardless of racial, cultural, social, and economic differences to be participants in the reconstruction of urban life, in accordance with our commitment to our faith, and our commitment to our God;
Therefore be it resolved, that General Conference of The United Methodist Church,  
  I.    Make a binding commitment to Urban Ministry by
      A.             Requiring United Methodist Conferences to designate 80 percent of net proceeds from the sale of urban churches and buildings that have been dissolved, properties and endowments to be used for in the same community for urban ministry redevelopment, evangelism, mission outreach. Such funds will be held in escrow for urban ministry purposes only; and
B.             that the remaining 20 percent be used to establish a national urban ministry endowment fund, which would be managed by the United Methodist Foundation, with program grants or loans from the fund’s income to be determined by the Urban Ministry Council;
                     C.   and that Urban United Methodist Churches and/ or agencies remaining in the community should be given the opportunity to expand their ministries through the use of the abandoned church and or/buildings;    
        II.    Direct the Board of Discipleship, General Commission on Race and Religion, and the General Board of Global Ministries, to make the problems of urban ministry an urgent priority and to implement jointly a national consultation, the results of which would be published and reported to the 2008 General Conference and to consider the social and economic problems of our Urban church communities in order to implement new strategies for addressing said problems.
      III. Call for a national year long celebration in 2005 of the United Methodist churches in urban communities coordinated by the Urban Ministry Councils, Board of Discipleship, and the General Board of Church and Society.
      
Submitted ByKathryn Cooper-Nicholas
Wilmington DE
