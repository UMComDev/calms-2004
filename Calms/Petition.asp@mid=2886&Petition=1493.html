Submitted Petition Text:
The General Conference of 2004 authorizes a task force to study the possibility of offering a minimum survival allowance for persons appointed in charge of congregations in central conferences.  The task force shall include two directors and two staff from each of the General Boards of Global Ministries and Higher Education and Ministry and the General Council on Finance and Administration, and two representatives from the central conferences.  The cost for participation in the study shall be borne by the three agencies for their own participation, with the cost of the participation of the central conference representatives being shared equally among the three agencies.
The agenda for the task force shall include:
  •          A survey of the frequency of lapses in salary payment to central conference pastors;
  •          The gathering of data from U.S. annual conferences which have initiated agreements with central conferences to assist in raising funds for pastoral compensation;
  •          An analysis of the extent to which the needs of pastors, which are addressed for those in the United States in ï¿½ 623 of The 2000 Book of Discipline, could be met in the context of the central conferences and/or in cases of severe economic hardship within the central conferences through action of the General Conference;
  •          Development of a means by which the proper qualification of potential recipients of the allowance could be ascertained;
  •          Contextual analysis of the various central conferences and their annual conferences, as well as cultural sensitivity among the many aspects of work, equitable remuneration, and survival costs; and
  •          Study of methods of international accountancy, transparency, and financial controls with particular attention to international systems of compensation; and
    The task force shall develop a report and recommendation to the 2008 General Conference to address the matter of a pastoral survival allowance for the central conferences.
