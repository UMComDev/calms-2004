Submitted Petition Text:
Make the following changes to the Staff Retirement Benefits Program (“SRBP”), effective January 1, 2005 (including any needed revisions to Program section numbering, pagination, or the Table of Contents):
Amend Section 2.17 of SRBP as follows:
  
“Employer” shall mean a General Agency, except The United Methodist Publishing House.
Amend Section 2.31 of SRBP as follows:
  
“Service” shall mean employment with the Employer  or any other unit associated with the denomination known as The United Methodist Church . Where the Employer maintains a plan of a predecessor employer, service for such predecessor employer shall be treated as service of with the Employer.
Add the following new Section 5.05a(6) of SRBP after Section 5.05a(5):
  
    (6)    A Participant who has received such explanation may waive the 30-day period and elect to have his or her benefit distributed immediately.
    
Amend the first partial sentence of Section 5.05b(1) of SRBP as follows:
  
purchase of or providing an annuity, for a period certain and/or for a period measured by the life or lives of the Participant and/or his or her Contingent Annuitant.
Amend the first partial sentence of Section 5.05b(2) of SRBP as follows:
  
payments over a period certain in monthly or annual cash installments over a period extending for the shorter of: (i) a period specified by the Participant (and approved by the Administrator), or (ii) the period ending with the installment payment that exhausts the Participant’s Account balance.
Amend Section 5.16 of SRBP as follows:
  
    5.16    Unclaimed Benefit.  The Administrator may prescribe uniform and nondiscriminatory rules for carrying out the following provisions:
        a.    If a portion (or all) of an Account remains to be distributed to a Participant, Former Participant, or Beneficiary at a time when it is due under the Program (including, but not limited to, the “required beginning date,” as defined in section 401(a)(9) of the Code) and the Administrator is then unable to locate such person, the Administrator will send notice of such benefit due by a certified letter with return receipt requested to the last known address of such person. If such person fails to contact the Administrator within 12 months (except as provided in Section 5.16b), such benefit will be forfeited (except as provided in Section 5.16c) and will become the benefit of, in the case of a Participant, Former Participant, or alternate payee (within the meaning of Section 9.04), such person’s Beneficiary, or, in the case of a Beneficiary, the Participant’s, Former Participant’s, or alternate payee’s successor Beneficiary (including any default Beneficiaries provided under the terms of the Program). The Administrator will then send notice by certified letter as provided above to the Beneficiary or successor Beneficiary (including a default Beneficiary), and the process specified above will be repeated until the last successor Beneficiary is sent a notification.
b.    If the last successor or default Beneficiary fails to contact the Administrator within 12 months after being sent notification of a benefit due as provided in Section 5.16a, then the amount specified in Section 5.16a will be forfeited. The Administrator will use such forfeitures to defray the expenses of administering the Program.db
c.    If, at any time before the expiration of the 12-month period described in Section 5.16b, a Participant, Former Participant, or Beneficiary who is or was due a benefit described in Section 5.16a claims the benefit, the benefit will be paid to such person (notwithstanding any previous forfeiture) if it has not previously been paid to another Participant, Former Participant, or Beneficiary. If the 12-month period described in Section 5.16b has elapsed, then such benefit will be permanently forfeited and used by the Administrator as described in Section 5.16b.
a.    If a Participant fails to properly claim a benefit due hereunder prior to the “required beginning date,” as defined in Section 5.05e(2), and there is no current mailing address on file with the Administrator, the Administrator shall send a certified letter with return receipt requested to the last known address of the Participant indicating that the Participant has 60 days to claim such benefit. If the Participant fails to claim the benefit within the 60-day period, the Participant shall be deemed, in accordance with rules and regulations adopted by the Administrator, to have relinquished any benefit that may be payable to the Participant.
b.    The failure of a Beneficiary to properly claim a benefit due hereunder during the stated time period, or if no time period is stated, then within two years of being eligible to receive the benefit, shall cause the benefit to be considered to have been refused and forfeited and shall cause the benefit to be paid to the secondary Beneficiary or default Beneficiary in accordance with the Program. If the last default Beneficiary does not claim the benefit within a two-year period commencing with the date on which he/she became eligible to receive the benefit, the benefit shall be considered to be refused and forfeited by said Beneficiary. After the last two-year period has expired, the Administrator shall send a certified letter with return receipt requested to the last known address of the last default Beneficiary indicating that the Beneficiary has 60 days to claim such benefit. Failure to claim the benefit within the 60-day time period shall cause the benefit to be forfeited. Such forfeited amounts shall be added to the reserves of the Program. However, any such forfeited amount will be reinstated and become payable if a claim is made by the estate of the Participant or Beneficiary. The Administrator shall prescribe uniform and nondiscriminatory rules for carrying out this provision.
        
Add the following new Section 5.18 of SRBP after Section 5.17:
  
    5.18    Forfeitures.  Notwithstanding Section 4.02, a Participant, Former Participant, or Beneficiary may forfeit an otherwise Vested Account in the following circumstances:
        a.    Missing Participant.  The Accounts of a Participant, Former Participant, or Beneficiary who cannot be located will be handled as described in Section 5.16.
b.    Uncashed Check.  Any Participant, Former Participant, or Beneficiary who has been issued a check for benefits due but who does not return or cash the check within a reasonable period established by the Administrator, after such reasonable notice (or in the case of de minimus amounts, no notice) as the Administrator may determine, will forfeit such benefits. Such forfeited amounts will be used by the Administrator to defray the administrative expenses of the Program. Uncashed checks returned to the Administrator because the payee is missing or for other reasons are not covered by this Section 5.18b.
c.    Relinquished Benefits.  If a Participant relinquishes a benefit as provided in Section 5.10, it is forfeited. The relinquished benefit will be handled as described in Section 5.10.
d.    Ineligible Employee.  Benefits credited to an ineligible Employee will be forfeited and handled as described in Sections 3.07 and 9.03b.
e.    Election Not to Participate.  Employees who elect not to participate in the Program under Section 3.08 will be handled as described in that Section, but contributions not received during periods when the election was in force will not be earned or will be permanently forfeited.
        
Amend Section 8.01 of SRBP as follows:
  
    8.01    Amendment of the Program.
  
        a.    General Conference.  The General Conference may amend prospectively or retroactively any or all provisions of this Program at any time by written instrument identified as an amendment of the Program effective as of a specified date.  However,
b.    Administrator.  The Board of Directors of the Administrator is authorized to amend any or all provisions of this Program at any time by resolution, effective as of a specified date: such written instrument in order  
            (1)   to conform the Program to any applicable law and/or regulations promulgated thereunder; .
              (2)    to conform the Program to The Book of Discipline or changes therein; and
              (3)    to take advantage of any permissive law and/or regulations promulgated thereunder, provided that such amendment results in no additional substantial long-term costs to the Employers.
              c.    Limitations.  Notwithstanding the foregoing, no amendment to the Program may decrease the balance in a Participant’s, Former Participant’s, or Beneficiary’s Account. Furthermore, no amendment to the Program may have the effect of decreasing a Participant’s, Former Participant’s, or Beneficiary’s Vested interest in his or her Account.
        
Amend the first sentence of Section 9.04 of SRBP as follows:
  
The provisions of Section 9.02 notwithstanding, all or part of a Participant’s v Vested benefits arising under this Program, including Supplement One, may be transferred to one or more “alternate payees” on the basis of a “qualified domestic relations order,” as those terms are defined in Code section 414(p), provided that (1) the Participant makes an assignment of benefits pursuant to the order, and the alternate payee accepts said assignment, on the forms provided by the Administrator; ( 2 1) said order was issued by a court having jurisdiction over the Administrator; or ( 3 2) said order was entered by any other court if the Administrator, in its sole discretion, determines that the order is likely to be entered by a court having jurisdiction over the Administrator.
