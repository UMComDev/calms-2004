Submitted Petition Text:
WHEREAS, there is a health care crisis in the United States; and
    WHEREAS, the Census Bureau sets the figure of uninsured persons in the United States to be 41.2 million in 2002, and the National Coalition on Health Care that includes former Presidents Jimmy Carter, Gerald Ford and George Bush, in a meeting on May 9, 2003, project that 53.7 million persons will not have health insurance by 2006; and
WHEREAS, the inability to afford health insurance by a large number of persons in U.S. society is affecting the general well-being of its people: and
WHEREAS, the inflation in health care and double digit increases in health insurance costs have been the norm in the Kansas East Conference and other United Methodist Conferences and is a reflection of the health care crisis in the United States; and
WHEREAS, the cost of health insurance is affecting the mission of The United Methodist Church; and
WHEREAS, the Social Principles of The United Methodist Church declares that "health care is a basic human right (161T);" and
WHEREAS, The 2000 Book of Resolutions, "103 Universal Access to Health Care," adopted by the 1992 and 2000 General Conferences, provides a sound Biblical and theological base in accord with the Social Principles to provide a basis of action for dealing with the crisis that now grips the U.S. society;
Therefore, be it resolved that the Kansas East Conference petition the 2004 General Conference to do the following:
        A.    To authorize the General Board of Church and Society with the Council of Bishops to create a task force to draft a method of health care reform for this society taking into consideration the welfare of every woman, man and child in the United States. This should be a totally ecumenical  and interfaith effort. And further that this plan be sent to every Conference and every United Methodist congregation in the United States with a specific plan of action.
            B.    The General Board of Pensions and Health Benefits to appoint a 20-member task force of the best minds and persons with the most experience to study and then create a plan of insuring the pastors and lay employees of The United Methodist Church with adequate health benefits at a reasonable cost. If findings between General Conferences can be included in HealthFlex, the General Board of Pensions be empowered to take action. A full and comprehensive report will be brought to the 2008 General Conference after consultation with the Annual Conferences.
