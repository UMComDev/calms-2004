Submitted Petition Text:
Each conference, under the direction of its bishop is allowed and encouraged to engage in a “holy experiment” to seek God’s will about how the church is to minister to gay and lesbian brothers and sisters by permitting one church in each conference to engage in the ministry of a transforming congregation and one church in each conference to engage in the ministry of a reconciling congregation. This holy experiment would take place over a ten year period, so as to discern both short-term and long-term implications of these two ministries. All clergy and laity would be encouraged to be in earnest prayer during this time period, asking for clear discernment regarding God’s will about how the United Methodist Church should minister to gay and lesbian brothers and sisters. The bishop will appoint a panel of twelve persons (six clergy, six lay) from her or his conference who would monitor the two churches. At the end of the ten year period, each panel would send one clergy and one lay person to meet with their counterparts, discuss what they had observed, and write up a report to be submitted to the bishops. The bishops would prayerfully consider the report and come to the General Conference of 2016, with a recommendation about how they believe the church should be in ministry. General Conference would then vote upon that recommendation, hopefully having come to a consensus about God’s will in this issue.
