Report of the Recommendation of GCOM and GCFA Regarding the World Service Fund Allocations to the General Program Agencies for the 2005-2008 Quadrennium

In April 2003 the General Council on Ministries (GCOM) recommended to the General Council on Finance and Administration (GCFA) a minimum of $230 million to represent the amount of funding from the World Service Fund to cover the minimum needs of the eight general program agencies and the United Methodist Youth Organization for 2005-2008.
  
At its May 2003 meeting, the GCFA set $222 million as the amount for the general program agencies for the 2005-2008 quadrennium from the World Service Fund contingent on the program agencies providing information that all missional objectives as outlined in the Book of Discipline are retained, operations are streamlined with reduced administrative costs, and demonstrate that they are working on addressing priorities in a long-range manner.
  
The GCOM and the general program agencies developed an extensive report for the GCFA documenting the painful reductions in the employed workforce that had already been implemented. Further efforts were documented of how administrative and organizational costs had been reduced through added efficiencies and through collaborative efforts among the general agencies. With the general secretaries and other representatives from the general program agencies in attendance, this information was presented by GCOM to the GCFA at a July 2003 meeting in Romulus, Michigan.
  
After further deliberations, the GCOM and GCFA program budget teams in August reached agreement to the following allocations to the program agencies of the World Service Fund for the 2005-2008 quadrennium:
  
   General Board of Church and Society    $11,700,000
    General Board of Discipleship    31,750,000
       Shared Mission Focus on Young People    3,100,000
    General Board of Global Ministries    126,000,000
    General Board of Higher Education and Ministry    25,700,000
       HANA Scholarships    2,200,000
    General Commission on Christian Unity and Interreligious Concerns    5,500,000
    General Commission on Religion and Race    7,000,000
       Minority Group Self-Determination Fund    3,450,000
       Project Equality    160,000
    General Commission on Status and Role of Women    3,040,000
    General Commission on United Methodist Men    1,500,000
    United Methodist Youth Organization    900,000
  
   Total    $222,000,000

This decision concerning the allocations was recommended by the budget teams and adopted at the joint meeting of the full membership of the General Council on Ministries and the General Council on Finance and Administration in Los Angeles, California in September. The specific detail of those actions is contained in the attachment to this Report.
  
Accordingly, both of these Councils (GCOM and GCFA) have agreed with each other concerning their recommendation to the 2004 General Conference for allocations to the eight general program agencies and the United Methodist Youth Organization for the 2005-2008 quadrennium as required in Paragraph 806.1b4 and 906.2d of the Book of Discipline 2000.
