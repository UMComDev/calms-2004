Submitted Petition Text:
Replace Resolution 235, Gun Violence, with the following:
  
VIOLENCE IN SOCIETY  
  
Violence and, more particularly, violence to children and youth is a primary concern for United Methodists. We recognize and deplore violence that kills and injures. In the name of Christ, who came so that people might know abundant life, we call upon the church to affirm its faith through vigorous efforts to curb and eliminate violence in all forms.
  
Violence is killing God’s people. The risk of violence to children and youth is more prevalent in the United States today than in any previous generation. Our communities and schools are exposed to increasing amounts of violence. No appeals to individual autonomy are sufficient to justify our church’s ignorance of the threat of violence to modern society. The need to prevent the incidence of violence and death resulting thereof is an issue of increasing concern. We as United Methodists call for social policies that seek to identify the base sources of violence such as intimidation and others and to promote programs and policies to help eliminate violence.
  
The United Methodist Church supports ministries that address the issue of violence and crime prevention.  Acts of violence should not be an acceptable occurrence in any community: suburban, urban, or rural. The church must continue to address these issues of violence and to develop programs to enrich the lives of everyone regardless of age but particularly of children/youth.
  
In light of the increase of violence affecting society, we call upon The United Methodist Church and its governing boards to:
(1) convene workshops of clergy and mental health care professionals from urban, rural, and suburban communities in order to discuss ways by which The United Methodist Church should respond and to determine what role the church should take in facilitating dialogue to address the issue of violence in our schools and in our communities; and
(2) educate the United Methodist community (parents, children, and youth) on weapon safety, violence prevention, and the public health impact of violence; and
(3) identify community-based, state, and national organizations working on the issue of violence and seek their assistance to design and support education and prevention workshops around the issue of violence and its effect on children and youth; and
(4) support existing legislation and enforcement of appropriate instant background investigations prior to gun purchases; and
(5) call upon the print, broadcasting, and electronic media to refrain from promoting violence and to "put to silence the ignorance of foolish men -- as free, yet not using [their] liberty as a cloak, for vice" (I Peter 2:15-16); and
(6) discourage the graphic depiction and glorification of violence by the entertainment industry, which greatly influences our society, and recommend that these issues be addressed through education and consciousness raising; and
(7) call upon the federal and state governments to provide significant assistance to victims of violence and their families; and
(8) support only those organizations whose agendas promote violence prevention and
(9) urge members of gun clubs and associations, particularly those who are United Methodists, to continue to expand their educational programs on gun safety; and  
10) recommend that annual conferences make visible public witness to the sin of violence and to the hope of community healing.
Submitted ByJohn R. Wilson
PA
Western Pennsylvania Annual Conference (Annual Conference)
