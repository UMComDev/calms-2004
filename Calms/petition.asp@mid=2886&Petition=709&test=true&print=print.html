Submitted Petition Text:
Whereas, the 2004 General Conference of the United Methodist Church has taken notice of and counsel from the Resolution on Marriage and Family Ministries adopted by our Council of Bishops in November 2001 and fully shares the concerns and objectives expressed in that Resolution; therefore
Be It Resolved that the 2004 General Conference of the United Methodist Church includes in its response to our Bishops the General Conference's strong encouragement for United Methodist Conferences, churches and appropriate United Methodist-related entities to:
  Pro-actively prioritize marriage and family ministries that nurture those relationships as core values of the United Methodist Church,
      
  • Identify and embed meaningful ways and means intentionally to increase respect and value among United Methodists for marriages, families and Christian home-making as primary Christian vocations and ministries,
  • Assemble and utilize more effective laity-friendly resources that facilitate biblically-based skills for intentionally engendering healthier family relationships, and  
    Be It Resolved that the 2004 General Conference agrees with the socio-political and legal affirmation of marriage as being only the union of one man and one woman and supports the adoption of an amendment to the Constitution of the United States of America that clearly defines marriage as a monogamous, heterosexual union.
