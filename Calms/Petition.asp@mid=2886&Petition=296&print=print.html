Submitted Petition Text:
Amend �359.1d(1) as follows:
  
    (1) Judicial Complaint-If the bishop determines that the complaint is based on allegations of one or more offenses listed in � 2702.1, the bishop may refer the complaint to the counsel for the Church, who shall be appointed by the bishop  elected by the clergy session of the annual conference. The counsel for the Church shall be a clergyperson in full connection and shall have the right to choose one assistant counsel without voice who may be an attorney. The elected counsel may appoint associate counsels who are clergypersons in full connection to oversee and present cases on behalf of the church.  The counsel for the Church shall draft and sign a judicial complaint, attaching as exhibits all relevant written materials, including but not limited to information from the supervisory process and a suggested list of witnesses as deemed appropriate, forward the judicial complaint to the committee on investigation and represent the Church in the judicial process. The statute of limitations in � 2702.4 should be considered prior to the referral of a judicial complaint.
