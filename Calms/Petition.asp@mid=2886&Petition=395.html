Submitted Petition Text:
Amend �820.1 as follows:
  
1. Of the total money raised in each annual conference for the Ministerial Education Fund, 25 percent shall be retained by the annual conference that raised it, to be used in its program of ministerial education as approved by the annual conference and administered through its board of ordained ministry. The board of ordained ministry will confer concerning use of the ministerial education fund. Administrative costs of the board of ordained ministry shall be a claim on the conference operating budget and no conference shall hold Ministerial Education Fund reserves in excess of $50,000 or an amount equivalent to 50% of the previous year's MEF apportionment receipts, whichever amount is smaller.  Conferences with reserve funds in excess of these limits will expend all of these excess funds over the course of that quadrennium for scholarship assistance to candidates enrolled in University Senate approved schools of theology with priority given to students enrolled in United Methodist Schools of Theology.  No annual conference that had been participating in a 1 percent plan or other conference program of ministerial student scholarships and loan grants prior to the establishment of this fund shall receive less for this purpose than it received in the last year of the quadrennium preceding the establishment of the fund, provided the giving from that conference for ministerial education does not fall below the level achieved in the quadrennium preceding the establishment of the fund.
    a) “Service Loans” from the various conferences’ portion of the Ministerial Education Fund may be considered repaid if the recipients served five years in the connection in appointments approved by their bishop.
b) "Service Loans" from the various conferences' portion of the Ministerial Education Fund, for students who serve in areas designated by the Episcopal Leader as "impoverished" and where the salary for this appointment is in the bottom quartile of annual conference salaries, may be considered repaid if the recipients served three years in the connection in an appointment approved by their bishop.
b c) In case the recipients of these loans do not satisfy the terms of the “Service Loans” by service in the “connection,” they would make arrangements to repay the loans with the conferences from which they received their loans.
