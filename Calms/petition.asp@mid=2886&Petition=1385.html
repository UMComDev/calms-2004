Submitted Petition Text:
WHEREAS, Hispano/Latino immigrants are a recognized and indispensable labor force of the United States economy; and,
WHEREAS, Hispano/Latino immigrants are, generally, underpaid or, sometimes, not paid for their labor; and,
WHEREAS, some national and state laws prohibit Hispano/Latinos from obtaining driver's licenses, which makes them often dependent on questionable or irreputable persons; and,
WHEREAS, Hispano/Latinos are often separated by deportation from their American born children and/or other family members with legal permanent resident status; and,
WHEREAS, Hispano/Latino are often denied access to adequate health and medicare services;
Therefore, be it resolved, that the General Conference demand that the United States government promote a general amnesty law to legalize the status of the many undocumented immigrants living and working in the United States.
Be it further resolved that, the General Conference demand, by way of the General Board of Church and Society, that the government require that employers provide adequate health care and encourage employers to provide other social benefits provided by public and private agencies.
Submitted ByMary Silva
San Marcos TX
Methodists Associated to Represent the Cause of Hispanic Americans
