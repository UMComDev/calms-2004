General Conference Lay Address
WHEREAS, every four years a layperson is given the opportunity to address the General Conference; and
WHEREAS, at the present time this selection is made from among conference lay leaders; and
WHEREAS, the great majority of competent laypersons are thus not given the opportunity to try out for that address;
Therefore, be it resolved, that each annual conference's lay council set up a process whereby any layperson who is a member of The United Methodist Church can try out to give the General Conference Address;
And be it further resolved, that each annual conference then submit that conference's winner to the General Board of Discipleship;
And be it further resolved, that the General Board of Discipleship set up a process to choose the General Conference lay speaker from among those submitted by the annual conferences.
And be it further resolved, that the General Board of Discipleship set up the deadline schedule to receive the annual conference submissions.
Submitted ByAnn Freeman Price
South Nyack NY
