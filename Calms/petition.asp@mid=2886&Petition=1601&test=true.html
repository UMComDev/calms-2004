Report No.21


REPORT OF THE TASK FORCE ON MULTI-LANGUAGE RESOURCES
  
Background and Mandate
During the 1997-2000 quadrennium, the General Council on Ministries (GCOM) sponsored along with
the General Board of Discipleship (GBOD) and the General Board of Global Ministries (GBGM), a
Spanish Language Writers Workshop. Because of its success and recognizing the global nature of the
church, a petition was presented to the 2000 General Conference which called upon GCOM to establish a
Task Force on Multi-Language Resources and sponsor a Multi-Language Writers Workshop inclusive of
Spanish, French, and German languages. The petition proposed an expanded event be held during the
2001-2004 quadrennium to provide training for Korean, Portuguese, French and Spanish writers in
writing church resources. The petition was approved by the 2000 General Conference, but without
funding to hold such an event.

Process and Implementation
The GCOM organized a task force that included GCOM members and staff and representatives from
GBOD, GBGM, United Methodist Communications and the United Methodist Publishing House. The
task force reviewed the Spanish Language Writers Workshop held last quadrennium, set the purposes and
goals for a multi-language workshop, developed a budget, established criteria for the selection of
participants, and selected leadership and resources for the event. The workshop was designed to train 80
persons; 20 in each of the four languages (French, Korean, Portuguese, and Spanish). Twenty of the
proposed 80 participants were to be from central conferences.
Because the General Conference petition was approved without funding, the Task Force requested
GCOM to prepare a proposal to request funding through the General Council on Finance and
Administration for contingency funds. This funding proposal was presented to GCFA but was not
approved.
  
The Multi-Language Writers Workshop was not held due to the unavailability of any funding source. The
GCOM Conference Relationship Work Area requested that funding for this workshop be included in
GCOM’s 2005-2008 quadrennial budget request. If the budget request is approved, the proposed
workshop will be held in the 2005-2008 quadrennium.
