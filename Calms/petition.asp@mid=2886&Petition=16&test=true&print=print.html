Submitted Petition Text:
  WHEREAS, the Hispanic/Latino population represent the largest and the fastest-growing minority group in the U.S.; and

WHEREAS, the Hispanic/Latino community has the lowest educational attainment and is among the poorest in the nation; and  
    
    WHEREAS, Hispanic/Latino students are continuously forced to drop out from school and join the labor force to help support their struggling families here and south of the border; and
  
WHEREAS, Hispanic/Latino students lack sufficient resources to finance their college education and advance their social status inside and outside the church; and
  
    WHEREAS, the Hispanic/Latino presence is under-represented in colleges and universities, especially in United-Methodist related institutions of higher education; and
  
    WHEREAS, education is one of the best strategies to achieve upward social mobility and one of the most important factors influencing the well-being of individuals, their communities, and the nation; and
  
WHEREAS, many Hispanic/Latino persons in the United Methodist Church have expressed desire to go college but lack the means to make this dream come true; and
  
WHEREAS, there is an urgent need to have a seminary prepared cadre of Hispanic/Latino pastors; and
  
    WHEREAS, the United Methodist Church has a historic track record of supporting higher education and showing solidarity to "the least, the lost, and the last" in our society; and
  
WHEREAS, the HANA Scholarship Fund, even when helpful, has proven limited for the growing Hispanic/Latino student population; and
  
    WHEREAS, the United Methodist Church can improve its support to help meet the educational needs of the Hispanic/Latino population nationwide;
Therefore,  be it resolved, that the 2004 General Conference authorizes the General Board of Higher Education and Ministry in consultation with the National Plan for Hispanic/Latino Ministry and the national Hispanic/Latino caucus MARCHA, to develop a plan for the creation of a “United Methodist Hispanic/Latino College Fund”.  This plan will to be presented before the 2008 General Conference for its consideration.
  
Submitted ByJoel Rodriguez
Norcross GA
North Georgia Committee on Hispanic Church Development
