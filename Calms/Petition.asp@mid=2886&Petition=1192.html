Submitted Petition Text:
WHEREAS the 1996 General Conference passed a resolution (Chungshindae/
Comfort  Women”/Sex Slaves Drafted by Japan During World War II (The Book of Resolutions of the  United Methodist Church, 1996) supporting the
activities of the Korean Council for the  Women Drafted for Sexual Slavery by Japan at the UN Commission on Human Rights  (CHR) on behalf of survivors of the atrocity; and
WHEREAS the report of the Special Rapporteur on Violence Against Women, Radhika  Coomaraswamy, on Japan’s military sexual slavery
(E/CN,4/1996/53/Add.1) passed at the  52nd session of the UN (CHR); and
WHEREAS the report recommended the Japanese government: (1) recognize Japan ’s  military sexual slavery as a war crime and a crime against humanity; (2) officially  apologize to individual survivors by passing a bill at the Japanese Diet, and (3) pay reparation to the individual survivors; and
WHEREAS the Japanese government has continuously been denying the passage of the report of the Special Rapporteur; and
WHEREAS all the non-governmental organizations (NGO) representing the military sexual slavery survivors and all of the governments of
Asian/Pacific nations affected, declared in Geneva that they would not accept the Japanese government-initiated  “comfort”/sympathy money collected from private citizens and business corporations, which is ironically called “Asian Women’s Peace Fund” (UN E/C.-24/9/2001-,12/1/Add.67),   and
WHEREAS in April 2003, at the 59th session of the CHR, the Special Rapporteur on Violence against Women, Radhika  Coomaraswamy, updated information on the issue, stating that Japan is continuing to refuse  implementation of the recommendations made by the international community  (E/CN.4/2003/75/Add.1-4), and
WHEREAS Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination Against Women (CEDAW), in its 29th session, held in July 2003 in New York, recommended that the Government of Japan  bring forth a ‘lasting solution’ to the issue of the war-time military sexual slavery /C/2003/II/Crp.3/Add.1/Rev.1), and
WHEREAS the government of Japan not only continuously refuses to accept the recommendations of the UN body on the issue of reparation for the war-time sex slaves,  but also repeatedly makes omissions and/or
distortions in history textbooks, and
WHEREAS the International Labour Office (ILO) Committee of Experts on the  Application of Conventions and Recommendations, in its report to the  International Labour Conference in 1996, said that the war-time sexual slavery  constituted a violation of the Convention 29 (Forced  Labour), and called on the  government of Japan to take action to remedy the  violation;
  Therefore, be it resolved that the General Conference 2004, in support of  justice for the military sexual slavery survivors: 1) urge Japan to accept and follow the aforementioned recommendations made by the CHR/CEDAW/ILO  by delegating responsibility of writing letters to the Prime Minister Koizumi  Junichiro - to the National Association of Korean American United Methodists (the National Korean  Caucus), the General Board of Church and Society, the General Commission  on Religion and Race, and the General Commission on the Status and Role of Women.
