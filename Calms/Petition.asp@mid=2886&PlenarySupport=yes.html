Please note: Petitions themselves are not voted on by the Plenary. Petitions are reviewed by legislative committees, which recommend concurrence or non-concurrence with the petition. Legislative committees may also amend petitions before recommending them to the plenary. These recommendations are reported as Calendar Items. It is the committee's recommendation - the Calendar Item - that is voted upon by the Plenary.

For a petition to be displayed here, the legislative committee assigned to review the petition must recommend concurrence AND the plenary must concur with that recommendation.

In addition, if a legislative committee recommends non-concurrence, but the plenary disagrees with the committee (and votes not to concur with the committee's recommendation), the petition will be displayed here. However, if the plenary disagrees (votes non-concurrence) with a Consent Calendar "C" (recommend non-concurrence), those petitions will not be displayed here.

Even though a petition is listed here, the text of the petition - and therefore it's meaning - may have been amended by either the legislative committee, the plenary, or both. Please click on the petition name or number to see any amendments.
