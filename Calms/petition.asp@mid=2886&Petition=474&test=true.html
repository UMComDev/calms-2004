Submitted Petition Text:
Amend �164.F as follows:
  
F) Criminal and Restorative Justice-To protect ....
We reject all misuse of these mechanisms, including their use for the purpose of revenge or for persecuting or intimidating those whose race, appearance, lifestyle, economic condition, or beliefs differ from those in authority. We reject all careless, callous or discriminatory enforcement of law that withholds justice from all non-English speaking persons and persons with disabilities. We reject the use of capital punishment and urge its elimination from all criminal codes.  We further support measures designed to remove the social conditions that lead to crime, and we encourage continued positive interaction between law enforcement officials and members of the community at large.
In the love of Christ, ....
Most criminal justice systems around the world are retributive. These retributive justice systems profess to hold the offender accountable to the state and use punishment as the equalizing tool for accountability. The worst extreme of retributive justice is the institution of capital punishment, the practice of sentencing someone to death.  We oppose capital punishment and encourage overt action to change the laws of those governments currently practicing capital punishment.   In contrast,  We support the development of restorative justice systems which seeks to hold the offender accountable to the victimized person, and to the disrupted community, instead of to the state. Through God’s transforming power, restorative justice seeks to repair the damage, right the wrong, and bring healing to all involved, including the victim, the offender, the families, and the community. The Church is transformed when it responds to the claims of discipleship by becoming an agent of healing and systemic change.
