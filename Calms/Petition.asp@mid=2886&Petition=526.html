Submitted Petition Text:
WHEREAS, in the one, holy, catholic, and apostolic church of Jesus Christ “[t]here is no longer Jew or Greek, there is no longer slave or free, there is no longer male or female, for all of you are one in Christ Jesus” [Galatians 3:28]; and
WHEREAS, some of the images to describe the church of Jesus Christ are:  a body (1 Corinthians 12), a household or a family (Ephesians 2) and a flock (John 10).  All of these images picture unity amidst diversity in race, color, culture, gender, sexual orientations, social status as well as age; and
WHEREAS, if the church of Jesus Christ does not learn to live in harmony and peace in the midst of differences it is hurting no other than herself and that leads to self-destruction and failure to live according to her Head and Lord’s design and purpose; and
WHEREAS, the membership of The United Methodist Church [as part of the universal church of Jesus Christ] has become global and diverse in all aspects; and
WHEREAS, in order to be faithful and true to the design and purpose as the body of Christ, The United Methodist Church must learn to live in unity amidst diversity; making its diversity her strength rather than her curse and weakness;
Therefore be it resolved, that the General Conference is urged to implement a global and diversity sensitivity training program for all its ordained clergy (deacons and elders), probationary members, candidates for ordained ministry, local pastors and other church workers, and key lay leadership;
Be it further resolved, that annual conferences are urged to organize a sensitivity training program under the direction of their Boards of Ordained Ministry and Laity, together with the Office of the Bishop and Cabinet to assist the local churches to prepare before any cross-cultural appointment is to be made.

Submitted ByJ. E. Ryder
IL
Northern Illinois Annual Conference (Annual Conference)
