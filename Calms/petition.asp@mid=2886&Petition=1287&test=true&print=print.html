Local pastors who have reached the age of 35, completed the required undergraduate work, the Advanced Course of Study or the Master’s of Divinity degree, have served full time for 5 or more years or the equivalent to 5 full years, and agree to the itinerant system and United Methodist doctrine and teaching shall be accepted into full membership and ordained elder at the annual conference following the application to the Conference Board of Ordained Ministry.  No interview by the Conference Board of Ordained Ministry or probationary period will be required as the candidate will have been under the direct supervision of the district superintendent and district committee on ordained ministry, and been approved annually by the Conference Board of Ordained Ministry.  Local pastors who are serving at the time of the passage of this paragraph will be allowed to apply for admission to full membership and ordination as elder during the 2004 annual conference of which they belong.  In succeeding years the application deadline shall be the same as for others who apply for probationary or full membership.  This provision will be effective upon the close of the General Conference
