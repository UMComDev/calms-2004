Submitted Petition Text:
WHEREAS, the Churches' Center for Theology and Public Policy was established in1976 in Washington, D.C. as an expression of the vision of Bishops G. Bromley Oxnam, John Wesley Lord, James K. Matthews, and others, who saw the need for a thoughtful Christian presence in the U.S. capital to explore the relationship between Christian faith and momentous issues of public policy, and
WHEREAS, thoughtful, non-partisan theological analysis of issues of public policy continues to be a most important resource for Christian citizens and their churches and for ethically committed public leaders, and  
WHEREAS, a part of the funding this ecumenical Center was secured by liquidation of the assets of the Methodist Corporation that had been designated for a presence in the nation's capital, and  
WHEREAS, income from this fund has been provided to the Churches' Center by action of the General Conferences of 1976, 1980, 1984, 1988, 1992, 1996, and 2000, in amounts ranging from $40,000 to $60,000 per year, while leaving the corpus of the fund of approximately $1 million intact under the investment supervision of the General Council on Finance and Administration, and
WHEREAS, the Churches' Center, with modest resources, has been an effective catalyst for Christian thought on public policy issues, drawing upon the work of theologians, biblical scholars, ethicists, and church social action leaders of many denominations through the years, but
WHEREAS, efforts to secure funding from other denominations have not been as successful as the founers of the Center had hoped,  
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, that this General Conference, while continuing to designate income from the Methodist Corporation fund as financial support for the Churches' Center for the Public Policy, now stipulates that (1) this income must be at least matched by the cumulative support from non-United Methodist ecclesial sources and (2) the basic invested corpus must remain intact and under the investment supervision and policies of the General Council on Finance and Administration.
Submitted ByMittie T. Quinn
Dumbarton MD
Dumbarton UMC (Church)
