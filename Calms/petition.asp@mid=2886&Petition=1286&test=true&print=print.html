Amend �506 as follows:
  
�  506. Quorum-When the General Conference is in session, it shall require the presence of a majority of the whole number of delegates to the General Conference to constitute a quorum for the transaction of business; but a smaller number may take a recess or adjourn from day to day in order to secure a quorum, and at the final session may approve the journal, order the record of the roll call, and adjourn sine die.
The ministers and lay delegates shall deliberate and vote in one body; but if by privileged motion one-fifth of the members of the Conference so call, the lay and clergy members shall have their votes counted as separate classes, and no measure shall be passed without the concurrence of a majority of both classes.  Those motions selected for voting in separate classes which require greater than a simple majority to pass the Conference shall require that same majority in both the lay and clergy classes.
