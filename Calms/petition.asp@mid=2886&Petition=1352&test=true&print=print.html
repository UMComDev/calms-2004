Connectional Table
Name - There shall be a Connectional Table in The United Methodist Church,  
Purpose - The purpose of the Connectional Table (CT) is to empower the United Methodist people by directing and evaluating implementation of the mission of the United Methodist Church between sessions of the General Conference.  The functions of the Connectional Table are:
1. To ensure that evangelism and social action are united in the work of the church that empowers the United Methodist people.
2. To plan and coordinate an annual convocation where all United Methodists are invited to conduct the business of the Connectional Table, to receive training in specialized ministries, to consider the future of The United Methodist Church and to celebrate what God is  accomplishing through the connection.  
3. To establish committees to oversee all areas related to evangelism and social action mandated by the General Conference and to assign them their responsibilities.
4. To enable the flow of information and communication between and among congregations, districts, annual conferences, jurisdictions, central conferences, general committees, and the Council of Bishops and to establish efficient use of electronic media to enable broad participation by church members in discussion and decision making at the general church level.
5. To review and evaluate the effectiveness of the committees of the Connectional Table.
6. To provide services of futuring and research to all committees.
7. To cooperate with the General Council on Finance and Administration in developing realistic budgets for the General Conference.  
8. To develop and review personnel policies.
9. To provide for the staffing needs for the Connectional Table and all of its committees to assure the goals established by the General Conference are accomplished.
  
Incorporation -The Connectional Table shall be incorporated in such state or states, as the Connectional Table shall determine. This corporation shall be the successor corporation to all previously incorporated agencies that are subsumed under the umbrella of the Connectional Table.
Amenability-The Connectional Table shall report to and be amenable to the General Conference.
Organization of Connectional Table - 1. Membership
a) The voting members of the Connectional Table shall consist of a maximum of 132 persons as follows:
(1) 85 persons elected through jurisdictional and central conferences, one from each of the 64 annual and missionary conferences in the United States, three from each of the seven central conferences;
(2) Up to 14 bishops to include the President of the Council of Bishops, three bishops named at the discretion of the Council of Bishops to ensure a minimum of three bishops from the Central Conferences
(3) Up to 12 members at large for balance of laity/clergy, racial/ethnic background, gender and geography. The members at large shall be nominated by a nominating process as determined by the Connectional Table;
b) Vacancies shall be filled by a Connectional Table nominating process.
c)  92 people shall constitute the nominating committee one person shall be elected by each General Conference delegation during the General Conference meeting. In the case of a vacancy the bishop assigned to the annual conference where the vacancy occurs will name a replacement. The nominating committee shall review resumes submitted by any member of a United Methodist Congregation and propose members for all committees of the Connectional Table.
2. Committees--
a) The Connectional Table may establish or abolish any committee as long as it does so in a way that is consistent with the directives of the General Conference.  
b) The following evangelism committees are suggested by the Discipline:

(1) Committee on Church and Society with the responsibilities described in �1004

(1) Committee on Higher Education and Ministry with the objectives listed in �1405 and the responsibilities listed in �1406, �1410, �1411, �1412, �1413, �1415, �1416, �1417, �1418, �1421, �1422, �1423, �1424

e) At least two-thirds of the members of every committee must be people who are not members of the Connectional Table.
2. Meetings -  
a) The Connectional Table and all of its committees shall meet annually in October or November. At all other times the work of committees or the Connectional Table as a whole shall be conducted using telephone or electronic means. One more than half of the membership of the Connectional Table shall constitute a quorum at the annual meeting. Decisions that are reached using electronic technology require that half of the members of the Connectional Table agree to the decision.  
3. Officers - The officers of the Connectional Table and its committees shall serve for a quadrennium or until their successors are duly elected.
4. Internal Structure - The Connectional Table shall elect a member of the Connectional Table to chair each of the committees that are established. Each member of the Connectional Table shall be assigned to at least one committee in the evangelism area and one committee in the social action area.  
5. Staff - The Connectional Table shall establish a committee on personnel that will recommend the staffing needs for the work of the church. Each committee of the Connectional Table will determine its need for staff and present a staffing proposal with job descriptions and criteria for  
evaluating effectiveness. The staff person responsible for the work directed by a committee shall be called a "Director" (i.e. Director of Global Missions or Director of Discipleship) Only the Connectional Table will have a General Secretary. The Connectional table may also name additional staff to accomplish the work specifically assigned to the Connectional Table.  The report of the committee on personnel must be approved annually by the Connectional Table.
