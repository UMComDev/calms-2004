Amend �221 by adding 221.1 and 221.2 as follows:
  
    Section 221.1 Guidelines for Baptism of Marginalized Persons
The following matters shall be investigated by United Methodist clergy and the appropriate steps taken prior to baptism of marginalized people.  The parents or caregivers of children who request baptism should be consulted prior to performing a baptism.  Their wishes should be considered with gravity.   Every effort should be made to ensure their presence at baptism.  For adults, reasonable and diligent efforts should be made to gain the attendance of those considered “family” by the candidate.
In the event that the candidate attends worship regularly some place, he or she should be encouraged to be baptized there if the candidate expresses any intention of continuing to worship there.  In prisons, jails, and other confined residential settings where The United Methodist Church provides spiritual and devotional ministry, it is appropriate for United Methodist clergy to baptize persons in the context of such events.  Otherwise, some other regularly available Christian service should be sought as the context.  
Such persons shall be considered as baptized into the congregation to which the clergy is appointed only if there is some attachment to the congregation, such as regular ongoing outreach religious services provided by that church to the place of the candidate’s institutional residence. Furthermore that place of residence must be deemed to be a likely ongoing domicile of the candidate.  Otherwise, the considerations set forth below shall be considered.  
221.2 Missional and Transitional Baptisms
If the candidate is unchurched and unattached to any worshiping community and is likely to soon live in a location with a local United Methodist congregation, the person appointed to serve the congregation shall be solicited to perform the baptism and to bring laity from the congregation to be present.
If the candidate is unlikely to affiliate in the foreseeable future with any United Methodist congregation and the hunger for the means of grace is powerful, United Methodist clergy shall have authority to perform the baptism and issue a certificate.  Said baptism shall be deemed “Missional” and said candidate shall be so called on any church record.  A Missional baptizee shall not be considered as a member or preparatory member.
In the event there is some likelihood at some foreseeable time that a candidate will become involved in the life of some local United Methodist church, said baptism shall be “Transitional.”  A Transitional baptizee is not a church member, but shall be shown on the preparatory list of the United Methodist congregation that the candidate declares as most likely that she or he may some day unite with.
Finally, United Methodist clergy are instructed to use these guidelines understanding that they do not describe every situation.  They deliberately do not set hard boundaries.  The decision to baptize is left ultimately to the sense of call and integrity of United Methodist clergy.  The primary rule shall be an interest-balancing analysis of the need for and feasibility of guiding the candidate into the life of a stable and ongoing religious community, ideally a local church, versus the present spiritual need of the candidate to receive the means of grace.
