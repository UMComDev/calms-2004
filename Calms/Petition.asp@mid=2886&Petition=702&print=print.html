Amend �306 as follows:
  
� 306. Candidacy for Licensed and Ordained Ministry-A person, upon hearing and heeding the call to servant leadership through licensed or ordained ministry, shall contact the pastor of the local church, another ordained deacon or elder, or the district superintendent to begin the process as an inquiring candidate.
The following is the process through which a person moves toward licensing and/or ordination and conference membership, and is to be resourced by the General Board of Higher Education and Ministry. All persons entering into this process shall receive written communication about decisions made regarding the different stages of their candidacy.
1. The Inquiring Candidate - Those seeking information and counsel about licensed or ordained ministry in The United Methodist Church
    a) shall contact the pastor in the local church or another ordained deacon or elder  other clergy of The United Methodist Church; obtain and read the book The Christian as Minister (or an equivalent resource approved by the District Committee on Ministry) and discuss it with the pastor in the local church or another ordained deacon or elder  clergyperson of The United Methodist Church. Inquiring candidates should be encouraged to pursue their education at one of the United Methodist seminaries. The pastor shall inform the candidate about the steps and requirements toward ordination,  including the requirement that certified candidates must have been a member in good standing of The United Methodist Church for a period of at least one year immediately prior to the application for candidacy.    The candidate shall be committed to Christ as Savior and Lord, acknowledge a call by God to give themselves to called-out and set-apart Christian ministry, and desire to explore that ministry within The United Methodist Church;
b) Shall meet with their pastor or another clergyperson of The United Methodist Church to counsel and pray about licensed or ordained ministry, and present a written statement of their call to the clergyperson for discussion.    
c)  Young adults between the ages of 14 and 20  should work with their pastor through an appropriate  resource approved by the board of ordained ministry of the annual conference, or participate satisfactorily in a ministry exploration retreat approved by the board of ordained ministry, before meeting with the pastor or staff parish relations committee of their local church.  
               d)  Shall meet with their pastor and committee on pastor or staff-parish relations to share their written statement reflecting their call to ministry. With affirmation of the candidate’s potential for ministry, he or she proceeds to the exploration phase.
2. The Exploring Candidate--Those seeking to explore candidacy for licensed or ordained ministry:
a) shall have been a member in good standing of The United Methodist Church for at least two years immediately preceding the application for candidacy, including a year of service in some form of congregational leadership, either in that local church or one agreed to by the district committee on ordained ministry, provided that in the case of an affiliate member, there has been a consultation with and approval by the charge conference of the home church (this paragraph shall become effective at the end of the 2000 General Conference);
b) a) shall apply to the district superintendent in writing for admission to the candidacy studies; and
c b) shall be assigned as an exploring candidate to a candidacy mentor by the district committee on ordained ministry in consultation with the district superintendent and shall complete the preliminary studies within the candidacy guidebook that will focus on the spiritual discernment of the call , after proper registration through the annual conference candidacy registrar and the board of ordained ministry.
             c) shall spend one year in a ministry setting approved by the candidate’s pastor and district superintendent during which time the candidate will exercise servant leadership  in a supervised ministry setting in a United Methodist church, exploring whether he or she has the necessary gifts, graces, and promise for licensed or ordained ministry.  The candidate is to give an average of no less than fifteen hours monthly to the position.  The position may be volunteer or paid;
    d) Shall participate in a jurisdictional or annual conference-sponsored exploration retreat for exploring candidates;
e) Shall meet at least quarterly with a triad of persons for support, conversation, and evaluation during their year in a ministry setting. The triad shall include the candidate’s ministry setting supervisor, a member of the committee on pastor or staff-parish relations of the candidate’s church, and a layperson directly involved in the ministry being served by the candidate;
f) Upon completion of the year of service in the ministry setting, the triad shall provide a written report of its observations regarding the candidate’s effectiveness, areas of needed growth, and lessons learned by the candidate through this experience. This report is to be provided to the committee on pastor or staff-staff parish relations of the candidate’s church.

3. Declared Candidate for Licensing or Ordination--Declared candidates are those who have completed the exploratory process in � 2 and who seek to become certified candidates. They shall:
a) consult with the pastor and committee on pastor or staff-parish relations after formulating a written statement reflecting their call to ministry and requesting recommendation for certification. The candidate shall be interviewed by the committee on his or her statement and Wesley’s historic questions in � 305;
b) be recommended by the charge conference in accordance with the following method: A meeting for the purpose of recommending a candidate for ordained ministry shall be preceded by at least two public announcements and shall be held in the presence of the bishop, district superintendent, or an authorized elder, who shall counsel with those present regarding the ability and qualifications of the applicant and make plain the importance of such recommendation to the candidate for ordination. To be valid such a recommendation shall be: i) voted by written ballot by two-thirds of the charge conference present at this meeting, provided that in the case of an affiliate member there has been a consultation with and approval by the charge conference of the home church, and ii) have been graduated from an accredited high school or received a certificate of equivalency;
c) continue the candidacy studies for ordained ministry with a candidacy mentor.
    a) have satisfactorily completed a one-year term of ministry service in a supervised setting in a United Methodist church;
   b) have been a member in good standing of a local United Methodist church for a period of at least one year immediately prior to certification as a candidate;
               c) meet with the pastor and committee on pastor or staff-parish relations to review:   i. the candidate’s written statement reflecting how their experience in a supervised ministry setting has informed their call to ministry;  ii. the report from the supervisory triad; iii. the candidate’s statement answering Wesley’s historic questions in � 305.   Upon a written ballot by two-thirds of the members of the committee on pastor or staff-parish relations, the candidate is recommended for certification to the charge conference;
               d) be recommended by the charge conference in accordance with the following method: A meeting for the purpose of recommending a candidate for ordained ministry shall be preceded by at least two public announcements and shall be held in the presence of the bishop, district superintendent, or an authorized elder, who shall counsel with those present regarding the ability and qualifications of the applicant and make plain the importance of such recommendation to the candidate for ordination. To be valid such a recommendation shall be: i. voted by written ballot by two-thirds of the charge conference present at this meeting, provided that in the case of an affiliate member there has been a consultation with and approval by the charge conference of the home church, and ii. have been graduated from an accredited high school or received a certificate of equivalency;
4. The Certified Candidate--Candidates who have completed the requirements of �  2-3 and desire to be certified as candidates for ordination shall  complete the following requiremnets prior to scheduling their candidacy certification interview with the district committee on ordained ministry:
a) appear before the district committee on ordained ministry for examination;
b a) complete the psychological tests required;
cb ) submit a written response providing evidence of understanding and expectation concerning the following: i) the most formative experiences of your Christian life; ii) God’s call to ordained ministry and the role of the church in your call; iii) reflect on your year’s experience in leadership in the congregation; iv) your future usefulness as a minister in The United Methodist Church; v) your personal beliefs as a Christian; vi) your personal gifts for ministry; vii) how your personal relationships may affect your future ministry;
d c) submit a written response providing evidence of understanding the ministry of deacon and the ministry of the elder within The United Methodist Church;
e d) provide other information as may be required for determining gifts, evidence of God’s grace, fruit, and demonstration of the call for the ministry of deacon or elder; and
f e)agree for the sake of the mission of Jesus Christ in the world and the most effective witness of the gospel, and in consideration of their influence as ministers, to make a complete dedication of _themselves to the highest ideals of the Christian life as set forth in _�� 102-104; 160-166. To this end they shall agree to exercise responsible self-control by personal habits conducive to bodily health, mental and emotional maturity, fidelity in marriage and celibacy in singleness, social responsibility, and growth in grace and the knowledge and love of God.2
f) appear before the district committee on ordained ministry for examination and candidacy certification vote upon the completion of all written materials
g) continue the candidacy studies for ordained ministry with a candidacy mentor.
