Paragraph 655.  
  
    1.  Each district shall have a district lay leader elected in such manner and for such term as the annual conference may determine. If it so chooses, the district conference may also have an associate lay leader to be elected as provided by the annual conference.
2.  The district lay leader shall promote the ministry of the laity in all aspects of the life of the church. Such leader shall provide for the training of local church lay leaders and shall support the work of lay organizations in the district such as United Methodist Women, United Methodist Men, and United Methodist Youth.
3.  The district lay leader is a member of the annual conference, the annual conference board of laity or its equivalent, the district conference, the district council on ministries or its equivalent, the committee on district superintendency, and the district committee on lay speaking ministries.
4.  The district lay leader may designate persons to serve as proxy in any of the above groups.
  
  Para. 656. Organization
    1.  Each district shall organize in such manner as it chooses to carry out the ministry of the district through a district council on ministries or equivalent body. Such organization should be designed to effectively promote evangelism, missions, stewardship, ethnic ministries and concerns, and any other ministries adopted by the district conference.    
2.  The district conference shall elect a board of trustees to hold title to all district property, including camps, district office, and district parsonage. Such board of trustees shall carry out the customary responsibilities undertaken by trustees of local churches and annual conferences, but shall always be subject to the direction of the district conference.  
3.  Each district conference shall also elect a council on finance and administration, board of district stewards, or equivalent body to provide for the financial support of the district. Such body shall have the responsibilities of a) preparing an annual budget for the district; b) allocating to the churches in the district adequate funds to support the work of the district, including the provision of housing to the district superintendent; and c) making financial reports at least annually for presentation to the district conference and the district superintendent. All district funds should be accounted for by proper record keeping, and the financial body shall see that an audit is conducted at least annually. The report of the audit shall be reviewed with the district superintendent and reported to the district conference.
4.  Each district shall elect a district parsonage committee consisting of five or more persons, who shall be nominated by the district committee on nominations. If there is no committee on nominations, the district superintendent shall nominate persons to the parsonage committee. The district conference may also consider other nominations from the floor.  
It shall be the responsibility of the district parsonage committee:
a) to make recommendations to the district conference regarding the purchase, sale, or rental of a district parsonage.
b) to prepare an annual budget covering the cost of providing the district parsonage, which may also include utilities, insurance, and normal costs of maintenance. The budget shall be forwarded to the district council on finance and administration for its recommendation to the district conference.
c) to supervise the expenditure of funds allocated from all sources related to the provision of the district parsonage and to account for such expenditures annually to the district conference.
d) to give oversight in all matters related to upkeep, maintenance, improvements, and appropriate insurance coverages for the district parsonage.
