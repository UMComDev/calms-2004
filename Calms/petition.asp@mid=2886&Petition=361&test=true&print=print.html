�  1004. Responsibilities-The prime responsibility of the board is to seek the implementation of the Social Principles and other policy statements of the General Conference on Christian social concerns. Furthermore, the board and its executives shall provide forthright witness and action on issues of human well-being, justice, peace, and the integrity of creation that call Christians to respond as forgiven people for whom Christ died. In particular, the board shall conduct a program of research, education, and action on the wide range of issues that confront the Church.
The board shall analyze long-range social trends and their underlying ethical values. It shall focus especially on the persistant injustice of poverty that blights the lives of millions of employees who perform jobs essential to the functioning of society at pay insufficient to support a decent level of living for their families.  It shall acknowledge how other social issues, such as health, housing, hunger, and environment are rooted in poverty.  It shall explore systemic strategies for social change and alternative futures. It shall speak its convictions, interpretations, and concerns to the Church and to the world...
