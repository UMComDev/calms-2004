Submitted Petition Text:
Whereas, Ordination is an essential aspect of the ministry of the United Methodist Church.
Whereas, the skills, gifts, and dedication of pastors are essential for a congregation to work effectively.
Whereas numerous lay people have complained about an apparent lack of skill in some of their pastors,
Whereas numerous lay people are complaining that their opinions and desires are being ignored by the conferences,
Whereas some pastors are disillusioned with their seminary education,
And, whereas some pastors have serious difficulties with the ordination process itself.
Therefore, be it resolved that,
Within the next four years, the United Methodist Church will appoint a committee to examine seminary curriculum and the overall ordination system, then offer a report and recommendations at the next General Conference in 2008.
Specifically, this committee will investigate and advise as to matters of what courses are offered at seminary, what courses are required, and how we can improve on them.
The committee will also investigate how much input the local congregations have into the ordination process.  Why aren't on cite evaluations required in each conference?  Should the District Committee and the Board of Ordained Ministry be more vigilant in overseeing candidates for ministry?  Should lay members from the church be allowed to vote on a candidate's ordination?  What is the criteria the Board uses when assessing candidates?
