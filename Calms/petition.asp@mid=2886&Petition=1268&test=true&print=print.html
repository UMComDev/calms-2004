Submitted Petition Text:
We endorse the continuation of the Native American Comprehensive Plan for the quadrennium of 2005-2008, with the following proposed changes (in bold) in their petition under Task Force Voting Members and their workplan under “Leadership Development” and “Denominational Presence.”:
  
Native American Comprehensive Plan: Recommendations for Continuation in the 2005-2008 Quadrennium

The Native American Comprehensive Plan of The United Methodist Church shall be developed and directed by the Native American Comprehensive Plan Task Force (NACPTF).
The Task Force shall have voting members composed of:
  Two members from each of the five jurisdictions to ensure Native American input from the local church level.  These representatives shall be selected by the College of Bishops, in consultation with Native American United Methodist Churches and Native American United Methodist ministries in the jurisdiction; The two members from the South Central Jurisdiction shall NOT be from the Oklahoma Indian Missionary Conference.

          In the 2005-2008 quadrennium, the Native American Comprehensive Plan (NACP) will intentionally direct its focus around three areas:
       Congregational Development
        Strengthening existing Native Congregations, ministries, and fellowships.
        Developing new Native Congregations, ministries and fellowships.
       Leadership Development
        Strengthening Native Leaders for local churches and conferences.
        Training for local, district, jurisdictional, and national ministry.
       Denominational Presence
        Strengthening contributions of Native Leaders, Native Congregations, ministries
        and fellowships to The United Methodist Church.
        Continue to resource the Jurisdictional Native American Committees.
