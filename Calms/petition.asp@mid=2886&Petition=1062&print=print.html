� 653 In each annual conference there shall be one or more districts, each district having a district superintendent appointed by the bishop. The district superintendent shall carry out the responsibilities set out in Chapter Three of the Discipline. A district conference shall be held if directed by the annual conference of which it is a part or upon the call of the district superintendent. If it so chooses, the district conference may meet on a regular schedule with notices thereof given by the district superintendent. One of the principal responsibilities of the district is to furnish an appropriate parsonage or, if authorized by the annual conference, an adequate housing allowance to its superintendent.
    Purpose-The purpose of the district conference is to make disciples for Jesus Christ by equipping local churches for ministry and by providing a connection for ministry  between the annual conference and the churches in the district.  
� 654.   Composition and functions
    1.  A district conference shall be composed of:
a)  clergy members of the annual conference and lay members of the various district charges at the previous annual conference;  
                b)    the district lay leader;
c)    the presidents of the district United Methodist Women, United Methodist Men, and United Methodist Youth;
                   d)   the president of the district board of trustees;
        e)   the chairpersons of all district committees;
f)    the chairperson of the district board of church buildings and locations;
g)   the district treasurer;
h)   such additional persons as may be specified by the annual conference, giving attention to inclusiveness;
i)   each charge may elect one member of the district conference regardless of the size of its membership;
j)   each charge with a membership in excess of two hundred may elect one additional member of the district conference for each additional increment of one hundred members.
        
    All clergy members and full-time or part-time pastors shall have the right of vote in the district conference. All lay members of the conference shall have the right of vote provided they are active participants in a local church in the district.
