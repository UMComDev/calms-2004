Submitted Petition Text:
In order to improve mutual understanding and unity, the GCCUIC shall conduct an internal assessment of attitudes, social needs, interest and religious beliefs among a sample group of United Methodists, implement dialogues around the emerging categories, using non-stereotypical labels, and report its finding in the plenary session at the next General Conference. The sample should comprise of lay and clergy, reflect diverse demographics, be based on total membership in the US, be proportionate across jurisdictions, shall meet at least a minimum valid sample requirement based on total membership, be constrained by budget, and other relevant criteria.  
  
Such an assessment/dialogue process shall be implemented by GCCUIC in close relationship with each Annual Conference within the United States. The assessment would provide a fresh source of data and the dialogues would offer an opportunity for listening, understanding, and appreciation of the various ministry perspectives. It is hoped that some outside agency would be utilized to assist with determining the appropriate instrument, conducting assessment, carrying out dialogues, and interpreting the finding in a non-bias way. This assessment/dialogue project will be paid for with the allocation of $300,000 in additional funding from the World Service Fund to be administered by GCCUIC. Perhaps such an assessment of this type might be done ecumenically with other denominations providing the "outside agency" for one another.
Submitted ByHubert Ivery
