Submitted Petition Text:
A previously non existent study committee shall be formed to report to the 2008 General Conference.  This Committee shall consider various possible new formats for future local churches to effectively offer the gospel to the world, provide for a meaningful worship context, disciple people, and offer opportunities for people to act on their faith.  Many issues shall be considered including, but not limited to: house churches, building-less churches, selected appointed clergy as non-salary tent-makers who remain full elders, clergy teams, regional local churches, networked local churches, local churches based on and fitted to public school districts and identity, etc.  
    Because of the concern ethics and safety have on structure formation this group shall also consider issues related to child safety, resources and staffing, dangers enhanced by one pastor charges with limited people in local church facilities which may lead to unhealthy work habits, feelings of isolation, challenges arranging counseling for members of the opposite gender (and therefore less safeguards against inappropriate relationships the risks of which increase when no other people are near the counseling session), difficulty visiting with neighborhood children who visit due to child safety concerns, and liability (all of which offer a confusing witness).  The committee shall consider organizational concepts which enhance safety, healthy safeguards in counseling, good mental health of clergy and meaningful ministry to the community.  Proposed changes to the Book of Discipline shall be made as applicable to allow for transition to such circumstances.  
    Finally the committee shall recommend biblical, theological and organizational guidelines for bishops, cabinets and annual conferences.  They shall assess the fruitfulness of current local church expressions, potential for future fruitful endeavors and methods for transitions and change which honor history, look to the future, do not compromise safety, emphasize the connection and strengthen the general understanding of - and the leaderships’ prerogatives in - our missional, connectional and Episcopal identity as the United Methodist Church.  This that we might focus on the principles to preach “where there is most fruit” (1784 BOD, pg.4), and that there is “nothing to do but save souls [i.e. holistic lives]” (pg. 12) and do it well as good stewards of our time, talents and treasures as a church.

Submitted ByShawn Bishop
Mansfield OH
