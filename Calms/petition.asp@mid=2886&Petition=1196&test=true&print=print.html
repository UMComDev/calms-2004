Amend �258.1 as follows:
  
� 258. 1. There shall be ...
a) The committee on lay leadership ...
b) The committee shall recommend ...
c) This committee is to be composed of not more than nine persons of a growing and deepening faith who are able to provide insight for the senior pastor regarding gifts and interests of the laity in the local church, in addition to the pastor and the lay leader. At least one young adult elected by the charge conference shall serve as a member of the committee. One or more members elected by the charge conference may be youth. The pastor shall be the chairperson and shall bring theological insight to this group in order to guide the selection process.  . A layperson elected by the committee on lay leadership shall serve as the vice chairperson of the committee.
d) Each year the pastor shall assemble from among the membership of the local church this group of people.  This group may vary in composition periodically based upon the context, size of the local church body and spiritual growth of the people.   In order to secure experience and stability, the membership shall be divided into three classes, one of which shall be elected each year for a three-year term. To begin the process of rotation where such has not been in place, in the first year, one class shall be elected for one year, one class for two years, and one class for three years. Each year’s new class, and vacancies at the time of charge conference, shall be elected from nominees, from the floor of the charge conference and/or through the recommendation of the committee on lay leadership. Retiring members of the committee shall not succeed themselves. Only one person from an immediate family residing in the same household shall serve on the committee. When vacancies occur during the year, nominees shall be elected by the church council, or alternative church structure, with the permission of the district superintendent.
e) In the identification and selection process, care shall be given that the leadership of ministries reflects a growing faith in Christ, loyalty to the United Methodist Church, support of the local church through prayers, presence, gifts and service, an understanding of United Methodist history, theology, and polity, and inclusivity and diversity.
