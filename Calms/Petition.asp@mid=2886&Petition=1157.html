Submitted Petition Text:
WHEREAS, clergy and laity are elected by annual conferences to be the members of our General, Jurisdictional and Central Conferences, and  
WHEREAS these elections are vital to our life together, and
WHEREAS, United Methodist believe in an informed, enlightened mind as well as a heart warmed by the Spirit,
Therefore be it resolved that our Bishops and each annual conference, and the appropriate committees within those Conferences, such as Boards of Laity, or Boards of Ordained Ministry, or others,
Are hereby encouraged to provide voters, both clergy and lay, appropriate and timely information, and ensure that the election process is fully informed, fair, just, and accessible.
Further, we encourage an informed process that includes announcements about the election process, and consideration of an invitation for persons, laity and clergy, to declare their interest in serving if elected.
Further, when information is gathered and published about those who declare their interest, that information should be reasonably comprehensive, embracing something more that just the persons name and titles of positions held. Include also something about their thoughts and feelings on matters they consider important in shaping the environment wherein we do our ministry.  
Further, we encourage each annual conference to be thoughtful and resourceful given its own circumstances.  Consider publication by paper and /or electronically. Consider a time for introductions and brief talks at or prior to annual conferences. Consider the benefits of and the decorum for the distribution of literature. Consider the benefits and limitations of direct mail. Always respect freedom of speech, freedom of association and other civil rights of our people.  
Finally, we encourage the gathering and sharing of information between annual conferences about the various methods applied to assure a fair, just and informed election process. We encourage the General Council on Finance and Administration to coordinate the gathering and sharing of this important administrative work.
Submitted ByJoe W. Kilpatrick
GA
