Amend � 304.3 as follows:
  
3. While persons set apart by the Church for ordained ministry are subject to all the frailties of the human condition and the pressures of society, they are required to maintain the highest standards of holy living in the world. Since many United Methodists believe the practice of homosexuality is incompatible with Christian teaching, self-avowed practicing homosexuals1  are not to be accepted as candidates, ordained as ministers, or appointed to serve in The United Methodist Church.  When a minister has demonstrated in pastoral appointments gifts and graces within congregations and the trust and confidence of communities for seven years, then the minister acknowledges being a homosexual, the minister shall continue in full connection with no prejudice and appointed as before.
