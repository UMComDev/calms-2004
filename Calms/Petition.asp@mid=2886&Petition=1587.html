Report No. 7

GCOM AND GBOD COLLABORATE IN CREATING FULFILLING CHRIST’S MISSION
BIBLE STUDY
  
Mandate
The 2000 General Conference approved the development of a Bible study based on the initial biblical
texts and concepts of the reflection guide for Fulfilling Christ’s Mission in the Life of The United
Methodist Church. The purpose of the Bible study is to provide a reflective resource centering on our
biblical identity for local churches and annual conferences to explore what Christ calls them to be and do
and to fulfill Christ’s mission in their lives and communities in the 21st century.
The Bible study was to be developed collaboratively by the General Council on Ministries and the
General Board of Discipleship and to be made available to the church in the 2001-2004 quadrennium.

Process and Implementation
From the outset, the Bible study was developed collaboratively by the two agencies. A joint writing team
developed the Bible study, focusing on the five transformational directions of the Living into the Future
proposal, as approved by the 2000 General Conference. Further, the Bible study sought to incorporate the
Forums on the Future.
The Bible study can be found at www.gcom-umc.org/future along with archived versions of the Forum
on the Future web casts held in 2003.
