Submitted Petition Text:
Add a new paragraph after the existing �510 and renumber the remaining material as follows:
  
    1.  Name-There shall be a General Conference Connectional Table, hereinafter called the Connectional Table.   
   2.  Amenability-The Connectional Table shall report to and be amenable to the    
  
            General Conference.
  
    3.  Purpose-The purpose of the Connectional Table is to be the continuing planning and supervisory authority, the interim presence, of the General Conference between quadrennial sessions. It shall work with the general agencies, the jurisdictions and the Annual Conferences, and oversee on a continuing basis the implementation of the decisions and goals of the General Conference. It shall supervise, facilitate and coordinate the mission of the United Methodist Church as defined and limited by the actions of the General Conference and the Constitution.  
    It shall have no role during the sitting of the General Conference other than presentation of a report on the state of the Church, and other reports requested by the General Conference.
         4.  Objectives-The objectives of the Connectional Table are
        a.             To propose, receive and evaluate, or organize the evaluation of, topics of all kinds important to the life of our Church so that they may be   considered for action in an organized and broadly based manner at the General Conference.  
b.             To consult continuously with and advise the agencies of the Church in order to ensure and maintain a balanced momentum of current Church programs.
c.             To be a visible, responsible group speaking unofficially for the United Methodist Church between General Conferences. The Chair of the Connectional Table may speak, unofficially, for the United Methodist Church between General Conferences but shall, while in the Chair, take no position not clearly endorsed by the General Conference.
    5.  Responsibilities-The responsibilities of the Connectional Table shall include, but not be limited to, the following
         a.             The Connectional Table shall have no legislative power or explicit budgetary authority, but it shall share and cooperate with the GCFA in the preparation of proposals for the programs and budgets of all Church agencies for subsequent consideration by the General Conference.  
b.             It shall organize and encourage the study of questions of Church policy on important issues in society with the goal of achieving broadly informed positions for further evaluation by the General Conference.  The Connectional Table shall encourage the study and development of new ideas and programs by providing opportunities for discussion through both meetings and electronic means open to members of the Church. This may be done in cooperation with specific agencies of the Church or by the  Connectional Table alone acting for the General Conference.  
c.             It shall set the agenda of the General Conference under the existing Plan of Organization and Rules of Order and act as needed to facilitate optimal use of the time available to the General Conference.  The Secretary of the General Conference shall report to the Chair of the Connectional Table. All proposals and petitions from any source authorized by the Discipline to be submitted to the General Conference must first be submitted to the Connectional Table which shall forward all such proposals through the Secretary of the General Conference to appropriate committees of the General Conference, with or without endorsement or comment.
d.             All agencies of the United Methodist Church shall report, between General Conferences, to the Connectional Table as the functional extension of the General Conference and shall respond in a timely way to  all requests for reports and information on that same basis.
e.             It shall oversee the development of new programs and agencies that are approved and funded by the General Conference and report to the General Conference on their progress.
f.             It shall prepare a “Report on the State of the United Methodist Church” to be delivered at each General Conference.  
    6.  Organization-
        a.             There shall be 29 members of the Connectional Table. Twenty five members, five from each Jurisdiction of the Church, shall come by election by the sitting General Conference from candidates within the delegation of each Jurisdiction nominated by that Jurisdiction. In order to insure expertise and diversity, four members shall be appointed during that same General Conference by the Council of Bishops from the general membership of the United Methodist Church. Of those elected by the General Conference from each Jurisdiction, two shall be clergy and three lay. Two appointed by the Council of Bishops shall be clergy and two lay. Elections shall be held and appointments by the Council of Bishops made as the last business of each General Conference and terms shall last through completion of elections at the next General Conference.
    In order to permit immediate implementation of the Connectional Table at the close of the 2004 General Conference, under the constitutionally defined time constraints of that Conference, the members of the first, and only the first, Connectional Table shall be appointed in the requisite number and type from each Jurisdiction by the members of the Council of Bishops from that Jurisdiction.
The Council of Bishops shall elect the Chair of the Connectional Table from the membership of the Connectional Table on the last day of the General Conference.  Members of the Connectional Table appointed by the Council of Bishops shall not be eligible to serve as Chair, except in the special case of the first Connectional Table appointed in 2004.  No person shall serve as Chair for more than eight years. No officer of any Church agency reporting to the Connectional Table shall  be a member of the Connectional Table.  The Chair may be removed at any time by a vote of 2/3 of the Connectional Table and a majority vote of the Council of Bishops.
        b.             The full Connectional Table shall meet together at least quarterly. Business of the Connectional Table will however be conducted continuously through the employment of telecommunication and other agencies.
        c.    An executive group composed of the Chair, Vice Chair and one member of the Connectional Table from each Jurisdiction shall meet as called by the Chair.
d.    There shall be a permanent Executive Secretary reporting to the Chair and assisting the Connectional Table with the aid of an appropriate office staff in a convenient location. The office and support staff shall be full time in that location.
e.    The membership of the Connectional Table will serve part time, typically for a total of the equivalent of four to six weeks per year, including time at the central location and at their home.
